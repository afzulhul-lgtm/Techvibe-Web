Apple Shocks With Cheapest MacBook Ever at £599 – Launches Days After Bargain iPhone 17e

By Mike Reynolds Senior Editor
Published: May 06, 2026 Updated: May 06, 2026 9 Min Read

The tech world is abuzz with Apple's recent announcement of its cheapest MacBook ever, reportedly launching at a price point that undercuts its previous models, and closely followed by the release of the iPhone 17e, a more budget-friendly version of its flagship smartphone. This strategic move by Apple is aimed at capturing a broader market share, particularly among budget-conscious consumers who have been drawn to the brand's reputation for quality and innovation but deterred by the high price tags. The introduction of these affordable devices comes at a time when the global economy is experiencing fluctuations, and consumers are seeking value for money without compromising on technology and brand prestige.

The significance of Apple's latest move lies in its potential to redefine the budget tech market. Historically, Apple products have been positioned at the premium end of the spectrum, with prices typically starting in the higher range for this class of devices. However, with the new MacBook and iPhone 17e, Apple is signaling a shift towards inclusivity, making its ecosystem more accessible to a wider audience. This strategy could pay off, especially in emerging markets where brand loyalty is still in its formative stages, and consumers are more open to adopting new technology that offers a blend of quality, features, and affordability.

While specific details about the new MacBook and iPhone 17e are still emerging, estimates suggest that these devices will retain many of the features that have made Apple products popular, including high-quality displays, robust security features, and seamless integration with other Apple devices. The MacBook, for instance, is expected to come with a powerful processor, ample storage options, and a user-friendly interface that Apple is known for. Similarly, the iPhone 17e is rumored to feature an advanced camera system, long battery life, and the latest iOS operating system, ensuring that users have access to the newest apps and security updates.

The breakdown of features and specs will be crucial in determining how these devices compare to their predecessors and competitors in the market. For the MacBook, factors such as processing speed, memory, and display quality will be key, while for the iPhone 17e, camera capabilities, battery life, and storage options will be under scrutiny. Apple's ability to balance these features with a lower price point will be a significant challenge, as it seeks to maintain its reputation for excellence while expanding its customer base.

For consumers, the appeal of these devices will hinge on the value proposition they offer. With more affordable pricing, Apple is likely to attract not only first-time buyers but also existing customers looking to upgrade without breaking the bank. The company's strategy could also influence the broader tech industry, as competitors may feel pressured to revisit their pricing strategies to remain competitive in the market.

How It Compares to the Previous Model

Comparing the new MacBook and iPhone 17e to their previous models will provide valuable insights into the upgrades and potential downgrades that come with the more affordable pricing. Historically, Apple has managed to introduce new models that offer significant improvements over their predecessors, whether in terms of processing power, camera technology, or user interface. However, the challenge with the new budget-friendly options is balancing the cost with the features and performance that users expect from Apple devices.

For the MacBook, comparisons will likely focus on the processor speed, RAM, and storage capacity, as well as any changes to the display and battery life. The previous MacBook models have set a high standard in terms of performance and portability, so any compromises made to achieve a lower price point will be closely examined. Similarly, the iPhone 17e will be compared to its more expensive counterparts in terms of camera quality, screen resolution, and overall user experience.

These comparisons will be essential for potential buyers who are weighing their options between Apple's budget offerings and the premium models. The decision will ultimately depend on individual priorities, whether it's the latest technology, the best camera, or simply the Apple brand at an affordable price. Apple's strategy of offering a range of devices at different price points could help in retaining customers who might have otherwise considered switching to competitor brands for budget reasons.

Performance — What Tests and Users Show

Performance tests and user reviews will play a critical role in determining the success of the new MacBook and iPhone 17e. These devices will undergo rigorous testing by tech reviewers and users, who will assess their speed, efficiency, and overall performance in real-world scenarios. The feedback from these tests will provide valuable insights into how well the devices handle demanding tasks, such as video editing, gaming, and multitasking.

For the MacBook, performance will be a key factor, especially considering the device's positioning as a tool for both work and leisure. Users will expect seamless performance, whether they are using the laptop for office work, creative projects, or entertainment. Any lag, overheating, or performance issues could negatively impact the user experience and, by extension, the device's reputation.

The iPhone 17e, on the other hand, will be judged on its camera capabilities, battery life, and how well it handles daily tasks such as browsing, social media, and messaging. The device's ability to provide a smooth user experience, coupled with its affordability, will be crucial in attracting and retaining customers in the highly competitive smartphone market.

Key Takeaways

  • The success of these devices will depend on their ability to balance affordability with the features and performance that users expect from Apple products.
  • Comparisons to previous models, performance tests, and user reviews will be essential in evaluating the value proposition of the new MacBook and iPhone 17e.

Price, Availability, and Where to Buy

The pricing of the new MacBook and iPhone 17e will be a critical factor in their appeal to potential buyers. While Apple has not disclosed the exact pricing, estimates suggest that these devices will be significantly more affordable than their predecessors, making them attractive options for those looking for quality and brand prestige without the high cost. The availability of these devices will also play a role, with Apple likely to make them available through its official website, retail stores, and authorized resellers.

For consumers, knowing where to buy these devices and at what price will be essential in making a purchasing decision. Apple's distribution network, which includes both online and offline channels, will ensure that the devices are widely available, although availability might vary by region. The company may also offer financing options or trade-in programs to make the devices even more accessible to a broader range of customers.

Is It Worth Buying? Final Verdict

The decision to buy the new MacBook or iPhone 17e will depend on individual needs and priorities. For those who have been priced out of the Apple ecosystem due to cost, these devices offer a compelling entry point. They provide the opportunity to experience Apple's quality, innovation, and seamless integration at a more affordable price. However, for those who require the absolute latest technology, highest performance, or specific premium features, the budget options might not be the best choice.

Apple's move into the budget segment is a strategic one, aimed at expanding its market share and appealing to a new demographic. The success of this strategy will be closely watched, not just by Apple enthusiasts but by the entire tech industry. As the market continues to evolve, Apple's ability to innovate and adapt while maintaining its brand value will be critical to its long-term success.

By making its products more affordable, Apple aims to attract a new wave of customers who value the brand's quality and innovation but have been deterred by the price. The success of this strategy will depend on various factors, including the devices' performance, features, and pricing, as well as how they compare to both Apple's previous models and competitors' offerings.
