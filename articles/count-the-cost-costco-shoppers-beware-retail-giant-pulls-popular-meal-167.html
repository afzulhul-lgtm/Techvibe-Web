COUNT THE COST Costco shoppers beware – retail giant pulls popular meal from shelves over ‘serious and sometimes fatal’ infection

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: March 16, 2026 Updated: March 16, 2026 8 Min Read

Costco, the beloved retail giant, has issued a voluntary recall of one of its popular meal kits due to a potential salmonella infection risk. The affected product, a meatloaf with mashed potatoes kit, has been pulled from shelves across various states in the US. This recall has left many customers concerned and wondering about the safety of their food purchases. As a responsible and informed shopper, it is essential to stay up-to-date with the latest news and take necessary precautions to avoid any potential health risks.

Introduction to the Recall

The recall was initiated after Costco received notification from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) about a potential salmonella outbreak linked to the meatloaf kit. The CDC had been investigating a cluster of salmonella cases, and the evidence pointed to the meatloaf kit as the possible source of the infection. Costco, being a responsible retailer, promptly took action to protect its customers by removing the product from its shelves and issuing a recall notice.

The recalled product is a meatloaf kit that includes mashed potatoes and is sold in a microwaveable container. The kit was distributed to various Costco warehouses across the US, and customers who purchased the product are advised to return it to the store for a full refund. It is essential to note that the recall only affects the specific meatloaf kit with mashed potatoes and not other similar products sold by Costco.

The recall has raised concerns among customers, and many are wondering how this could have happened.

"Food safety is our top priority, and we take any potential risk to our customers' health very seriously,"
said a Costco spokesperson. The company is working closely with the CDC and other regulatory agencies to investigate the source of the contamination and prevent any future occurrences.

Understanding Salmonella Infection

Salmonella is a type of bacteria that can cause serious and sometimes fatal infections in humans. The bacteria can be found in various food products, including meat, poultry, eggs, and produce. Salmonella infection can cause a range of symptoms, from mild to severe, including diarrhea, abdominal cramps, fever, and vomiting. In severe cases, salmonella infection can lead to life-threatening complications, such as bacteremia and meningitis.

According to the CDC, salmonella is one of the most common causes of foodborne illness in the US, with over 1 million cases reported each year. The bacteria can be spread through contaminated food, water, and contact with infected animals or people. It is essential to handle and cook food safely to prevent the risk of salmonella infection.

The recall highlights the importance of food safety and the need for retailers and consumers to be vigilant about the products they sell and purchase.

Symptoms of Salmonella Poisoning

Salmonella poisoning can cause a range of symptoms, from mild to severe. Common symptoms include diarrhea, abdominal cramps, fever, and vomiting. In severe cases, salmonella infection can lead to life-threatening complications, such as bacteremia and meningitis. It is essential to seek medical attention immediately if you experience any of these symptoms, especially if you have consumed the recalled product.

If you have purchased the recalled product and are concerned about your health, it is crucial to contact your healthcare provider or seek medical attention immediately. Your healthcare provider can provide guidance on the best course of treatment and help you manage any symptoms. In addition, it is essential to follow proper food safety guidelines to prevent the risk of salmonella infection, such as handling and cooking food safely and avoiding cross-contamination.
