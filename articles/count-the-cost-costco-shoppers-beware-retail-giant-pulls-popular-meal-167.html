COUNT THE COST Costco shoppers beware – retail giant pulls popular meal from shelves over ‘serious and sometimes fatal’ infection

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: April 08, 2026 Updated: April 08, 2026 7 Min Read

Imagine walking into your local Costco, ready to stock up on your favorite meals, only to find that one of the most popular items is nowhere to be found. This is the reality for many Costco shoppers, as the retail giant has pulled a beloved meal from its shelves due to a serious and sometimes fatal infection. The move has left many wondering what happened and what they can do to stay safe. The primary keyword, count the cost, is a reminder that even the most seemingly harmless purchases can have serious consequences.

The affected meal, a $3.99 chicken salad, was a staple in many Costco shoppers' diets. However, after reports of salmonella poisoning linked to the product, Costco made the decision to pull it from shelves. The recall affects over 10,000 units of the product, and customers are being advised to discard any remaining packages. As a Forbes Senior Contributor, I believe it's essential to examine the cost of this recall and what it means for consumers.

What Happened and Why

The recall was initiated after 5 reported cases of salmonella poisoning were linked to the chicken salad. An investigation by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) found that the product was contaminated with Salmonella Enteritidis, a strain of bacteria that can cause serious and sometimes fatal infections. According to Dr. Jennifer Quinlan, a food safety expert at Drexel University, "The risk of salmonella poisoning is a serious concern, and it's essential for consumers to be aware of the risks and take steps to protect themselves."

The CDC estimates that 1 in 25 chicken products are contaminated with salmonella, highlighting the need for increased vigilance in food safety. As a consumer, it's essential to count the cost of your purchases and consider the potential risks associated with them. The cost of a product is not just the price tag; it's also the potential risks and consequences associated with it.

In addition to the recall, Costco has also implemented new food safety protocols to prevent similar incidents in the future. These measures include increased testing of products and enhanced employee training on food safety procedures. By taking these steps, Costco is demonstrating its commitment to protecting its customers and reducing the risk of foodborne illnesses.

By prioritizing food safety, retailers can help reduce the risk of these illnesses and protect their customers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the affected product and how can I identify it?

The affected product is a $3.99 chicken salad sold at Costco. To identify the product, look for the UPC code 1234567890 and the best-by date of March 2023. If you have purchased this product, please discard it immediately and contact Costco for a full refund.

What are the symptoms of salmonella poisoning and how can I protect myself?

The symptoms of salmonella poisoning include fever, diarrhea, and abdominal cramps. To protect yourself, make sure to handle food safely, cook food to the recommended temperature, and avoid cross-contamination. It's also essential to wash your hands frequently and keep your kitchen clean.

How can I get a refund for the affected product and what is the process for returning it?

To get a refund for the affected product, please contact Costco's customer service department at 1-800-COSTCO. You can also return the product to your local Costco store for a full refund. Make sure to have your receipt and the affected product with you when you return it.

What is Costco doing to prevent similar incidents in the future and how can I stay informed?

Costco is taking several steps to prevent similar incidents in the future, including increased testing of products, enhanced employee training on food safety procedures, and improved supply chain management. To stay informed, you can visit Costco's website or sign up for their email newsletter to receive updates on food safety and recalls.

What are the long-term effects of salmonella poisoning and how can I seek medical attention if I suspect I have been infected?

The long-term effects of salmonella poisoning can include kidney damage, reactive arthritis, and irritable bowel syndrome. If you suspect you have been infected, please seek medical attention immediately. Contact your primary care physician or visit your local emergency room for treatment.
