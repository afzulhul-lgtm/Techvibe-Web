How the Waree Solar Panel System Works

The Waree solar panel system works by converting sunlight into electricity using a process known as photovoltaics (PV). The solar panels are made up of many small PV cells, which are designed to capture the energy from sunlight and convert it into electrical energy. The electrical energy is then sent through an inverter, which converts the DC power into AC power that can be used in the home.

The system also includes a mounting system, which is used to secure the solar panels to the roof or ground. The mounting system is designed to be adjustable, allowing homeowners to optimize the angle and direction of the solar panels for maximum energy production. The system also includes a monitoring system, which allows homeowners to track their energy production and consumption in real-time.

One of the key features of the Waree solar panel system is its ability to work in tandem with the grid. During the day, when the system is producing more energy than the home is using, the excess energy is fed back into the grid, earning the homeowner credits on their energy bill. At night, or during periods of low sunlight, the home draws energy from the grid, ensuring a reliable and consistent supply of power.
