Maruti Ciaz luxury design sedan comes with high tec features, mileage is 20kmpl

By Sarah Mitchell
Published: March 04, 2026 Updated: March 04, 2026 5 Min Read

As I reflect on my first encounter with the Maruti Ciaz back in 2015, I am reminded of the impact it had on the midsize sedan market. With its spacious interior, refined features, and surprisingly fun driving experience, it quickly became a favorite among families. Fast forward to March 2026, and the whispers of its discontinuation have grown louder, marking what might be the final chapter for this Nexa icon.

Introduction to the Maruti Ciaz

The Maruti Ciaz was launched in 2014 as the successor to the SX4, and it quickly carved a niche for itself with its elegant design and roomy cabin. It outsold rivals like the Honda City and Hyundai Verna for years, crossing 2.7 lakh sales in its first five years alone. By offering a premium feel at accessible prices starting around ₹9 lakh ex-showroom, it appealed to buyers craving comfort over flash.

Sales Performance and Market Shift

However, the sedan segment has shrunk dramatically - from 20% of India's car market in 2015 to under 10% by 2024 - as SUVs surged past 50%. Maruti's Ciaz felt the pinch, with monthly sales dipping into three digits by late 2024, like 464 units in December. Reports confirm production halted by March 2025, with sales wrapping up by April, though Maruti called early rumors "incorrect".

Maruti Ciaz: Features and Specs

At its core, the Ciaz was about effortless driving. The 1.5-litre K15 petrol engine delivered 105PS and a smooth 20.65 kmpl ARAI mileage on manuals, making it a highway cruiser par excellence. Automatic variants with a 4-speed torque converter were no slouches either, hitting 20 kmpl in real-world runs. Space was its superpower, with rear passengers enjoying limo-like legroom and the 510-litre boot swallowing weekend luggage with ease.

Market Competitors and the Sedan Segment

In today's market, the Ciaz faced stiff competition from the likes of Hyundai Verna, Honda City, Volkswagen Virtus, and Skoda Slavia. These rivals offered turbocharged engines, advanced safety features, and modern tech like wireless charging. However, none matched the Ciaz's rear space or value, until SUVs stole the show entirely.

Final Days: Discounts and Dealer Stock

As of early 2026, remaining Ciaz stock lingers at Nexa outlets with hefty incentives - up to ₹2 lakh off in some cases. Base Sigma manuals go for under ₹9 lakh on-road, but act fast; inventories are thinning. Owners rave about its reliability - many clock 1 lakh km with minimal issues - and resale holds decently at 60-70% after three years.

Conclusion and What's Next for Maruti

No direct Ciaz replacement looms; Maruti's betting big on SUVs like the electric e Vitara and Victoris. The nameplate might revive in a crossover twist, echoing Baleno's evolution, but that's speculative. For die-hards, used Ciaz units under ₹5 lakh offer unbeatable value. Its exit underscores India's auto shift - SUVs rule, sedans fade. Yet, in a sea of high-riding crossovers, I'll miss that plush ride and cavernous back seat.
