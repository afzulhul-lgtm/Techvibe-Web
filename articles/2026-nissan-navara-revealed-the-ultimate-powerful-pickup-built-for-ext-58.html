The 2026 Nissan Navara is powered by a range of engines, including a 3.0-liter V6 diesel and a 2.3-liter turbocharged diesel. These engines provide the Navara with the power and torque needed to tackle challenging off-road terrain, with estimates suggesting that the V6 diesel engine produces around 190 horsepower and 330 lb-ft of torque. The Navara also features an advanced off-road system, which includes a rear locking differential, hill descent control, and a multi-mode 4-wheel drive system. This system allows drivers to switch between different drive modes, including 2-wheel drive, 4-wheel drive, and low-range 4-wheel drive, to suit the terrain and driving conditions.

The Navara's off-road capability is further enhanced by its approach and departure angles, which are reportedly around 33 degrees and 29 degrees, respectively. This allows the vehicle to navigate steep inclines and declines with ease, making it an ideal choice for drivers who need to tackle challenging off-road terrain. The Navara also features a wade-sensing system, which allows drivers to monitor the water depth when driving through flooded areas, providing an additional layer of safety and reassurance.

In terms of suspension, the Navara features a coil-sprung rear axle and a double-wishbone front suspension, which provides a comfortable ride and stable handling on both on-road and off-road terrain. The vehicle's ground clearance is also reportedly around 10.6 inches, which allows it to navigate rocky and uneven terrain with ease.
