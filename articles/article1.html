Eclipse of the century: nearly six full minutes of darkness when it will happen and the best places to watch mapped out

By Sarah Mitchell
23 Feb, 2026

The world is waiting for a rare celestial event, the Total solar eclipse 2027, which promises to be a once-in-a-lifetime experience. This eclipse will bring nearly six full minutes of darkness, a rare occurrence that will captivate astronomers and sky gazers alike. The path of totality will span across several countries, offering a unique opportunity to witness this phenomenon. On August 2, 2027, the moon will pass directly between the Earth and the sun, blocking the sun's light and revealing the sun's ethereal corona.

When and Where to Watch

The Total solar eclipse 2027 will occur on August 2, 2027, and will be visible from parts of North America, Europe, and Asia. The path of totality will pass through several major cities, including London, Paris, and New York. The eclipse will begin at 10:00 AM GMT and will last for approximately 5 minutes and 40 seconds. To witness this rare event, viewers should head to locations with minimal cloud cover and unobstructed views of the horizon.

Best Places to Witness the Eclipse

Several locations have been identified as ideal spots to witness the Total solar eclipse 2027. These include national parks, observatories, and other areas with minimal light pollution. Some of the best places to watch the eclipse include the Grand Canyon, Yellowstone National Park, and the Atacama Desert. These locations offer a unique combination of clear skies, scenic views, and minimal distractions, making them perfect for witnessing this rare celestial event.
