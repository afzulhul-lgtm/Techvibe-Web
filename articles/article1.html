Eclipse of the century: nearly six full minutes of darkness when it will happen and the best places to watch mapped out

By Sarah Mitchell
23 Feb, 2026

The wait is almost over for the most anticipated celestial event of the decade - the Total solar eclipse 2027. This rare phenomenon will bring nearly six full minutes of darkness, a sight to behold for astronomers and sky gazers alike. On August 2, 2027, the moon will pass directly between the Earth and the sun, blocking the sun's light and casting a shadow on our planet. The path of totality will stretch across several countries, offering a unique opportunity for people to witness this spectacle.

When and Where to Watch

The Total solar eclipse 2027 will occur on August 2, 2027, with the path of totality passing through North America, the Caribbean, and South America. The best places to watch this event include Mexico, the United States, and parts of South America, such as Brazil and Argentina. These locations will offer the best views of the eclipse, with the longest duration of totality expected in the western United States.

Best Viewing Spots

Several cities and national parks in the United States will offer ideal viewing conditions for the Total solar eclipse 2027. Some of the best spots include Carbondale, Illinois, and Casper, Wyoming, which are located in the path of totality. Other popular destinations include Grand Teton National Park and Yellowstone National Park, which will offer stunning views of the eclipse amidst breathtaking natural scenery.
