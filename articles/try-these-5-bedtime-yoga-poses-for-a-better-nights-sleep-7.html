Try These 5 Bedtime Yoga Poses for a Better Nights Sleep

By Dr. Lisa Park Senior Editor
Published: May 06, 2026 Updated: May 06, 2026 5 Min Read

Approximately 30% of the general population experiences sleep disruptions, which can lead to various health issues, including cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and obesity. The American Academy of Sleep Medicine recommends adults aim for 7-9 hours of sleep each night. Establishing a consistent bedtime routine, such as practicing yoga, can help improve sleep quality.

Research suggests that yoga can help reduce stress and anxiety, making it easier to fall asleep and stay asleep. The National Sleep Foundation estimates that stress and anxiety are among the top causes of sleep disruptions. By incorporating yoga into a bedtime routine, individuals can potentially reduce their risk of developing sleep disorders and improve overall health.

What Makes This Approach Effective

Yoga combines physical movement, breathing techniques, and meditation to promote relaxation and reduce stress. The parasympathetic nervous system, responsible for promoting relaxation and reducing stress, is activated during yoga practice. This can help slow down heart rate, lower blood pressure, and relax muscles, making it easier to fall asleep.

The brain's neurotransmitters, such as serotonin and melatonin, play a crucial role in regulating sleep-wake cycles. Yoga has been shown to increase the production of these neurotransmitters, which can help improve sleep quality. Additionally, yoga can help reduce symptoms of insomnia, sleep apnea, and restless leg syndrome.

Estimates suggest that approximately 55% of yoga practitioners experience improved sleep quality. A study published in the Journal of Clinical Sleep Medicine found that yoga practice was associated with improved sleep quality, reduced sleep latency, and increased sleep duration.

Step-by-Step Guide to Get Started

To get started with bedtime yoga, begin by finding a quiet and comfortable space to practice. Choose a yoga mat and any necessary props, such as blocks or blankets. Start with simple poses, such as child's pose, downward-facing dog, and seated forward fold. Hold each pose for approximately 5-10 breaths, focusing on deep, slow inhales and exhales.

As you become more comfortable with the poses, you can gradually increase the duration and intensity of your practice. It's essential to listen to your body and modify or rest when needed. Yoga is not a competition; it's about cultivating awareness and promoting relaxation.

A typical bedtime yoga routine can include 5-10 poses, lasting approximately 10-30 minutes. The following poses are commonly recommended for promoting relaxation and improving sleep quality:

  • Legs up the wall pose, which can help reduce swelling and promote relaxation
  • Reclined pigeon pose, which can help stretch the hips and lower back
  • Seated forward fold, which can help stretch the spine and promote relaxation
  • Child's pose, which can help promote relaxation and reduce stress
  • Savasana, which can help promote deep relaxation and reduce anxiety

How to Practice Safely Without Injury

To practice yoga safely and avoid injury, it's essential to listen to your body and modify or rest when needed. Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your practice. Avoid pushing past pain or discomfort, as this can lead to injury.

It's also essential to practice yoga on an empty stomach or at least 2-3 hours after eating. This can help prevent discomfort and promote relaxation. Avoid practicing yoga during extreme temperatures or when feeling fatigued.

Reportedly, many yoga studios and online resources offer classes and tutorials specifically designed for beginners and individuals with injuries or limitations. These classes can provide a safe and supportive environment to learn and practice yoga.

Key Takeaways

  • Bedtime yoga can help improve sleep quality and reduce sleep disorders
  • Yoga combines physical movement, breathing techniques, and meditation to promote relaxation and reduce stress
  • Start with simple poses and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your practice

Long-Term Benefits and What to Expect

Regular bedtime yoga practice can lead to long-term benefits, including improved sleep quality, reduced stress and anxiety, and increased overall well-being. Estimates suggest that approximately 70% of yoga practitioners experience improved overall health and well-being.

With consistent practice, you can expect to notice improvements in your sleep quality, such as increased sleep duration, reduced sleep latency, and improved sleep efficiency. You may also experience reduced symptoms of insomnia, sleep apnea, and restless leg syndrome.

Typically, it takes approximately 2-4 weeks to notice significant improvements in sleep quality. However, this can vary depending on individual factors, such as the frequency and intensity of practice, as well as overall health and well-being.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to practice bedtime yoga?

The best time to practice bedtime yoga is approximately 30-60 minutes before bedtime, allowing time for relaxation and wind-down before sleep.

Can I practice bedtime yoga if I have a medical condition?

It's essential to consult with a healthcare professional before starting a new exercise routine, especially if you have a medical condition. They can provide guidance on safe and modified practices.

How often should I practice bedtime yoga?

Aim to practice bedtime yoga at least 3-4 times per week, ideally every night, to experience significant improvements in sleep quality and overall well-being.

Can I practice bedtime yoga if I'm a beginner?

Yes, bedtime yoga is suitable for beginners. Start with simple poses and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your practice. Listen to your body and modify or rest when needed.

Conclusion

Bedtime yoga offers a natural and effective way to improve sleep quality and reduce sleep disorders. By incorporating yoga into a bedtime routine, individuals can promote relaxation, reduce stress and anxiety, and enhance overall well-being. With consistent practice and patience, you can experience significant improvements in sleep quality and wake up feeling refreshed and revitalized.

Remember to start slowly, listen to your body, and modify or rest when needed. Consult with a healthcare professional if you have any concerns or questions. With bedtime yoga, you can take the first step towards a restful and rejuvenating sleep, and wake up to a happier, healthier you.
