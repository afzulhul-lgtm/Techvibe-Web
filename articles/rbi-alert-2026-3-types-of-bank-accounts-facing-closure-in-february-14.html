RBI Alert 2026: 3 Types of Bank Accounts Facing Closure in February

By Sarah Mitchell
23 Feb, 2026

Are you aware of the recent RBI alert that could potentially close your bank account in February? The Reserve Bank of India has introduced new rules and guidelines for 2026, aiming to tackle inoperative and dormant accounts. If you're a bank account holder, it's essential to stay updated on these changes to avoid any inconvenience.

RBI New Rules 2026: What You Need to Know

The RBI has announced that three types of bank accounts will face closure in February, including inoperative, dormant, and accounts with incomplete KYC updates. This move is part of the RBI's efforts to maintain the integrity of the Indian banking system and prevent fraudulent activities.

Types of Bank Accounts Facing Closure

The RBI guidelines 2026 specify that accounts with no transactions for an extended period will be considered inoperative or dormant. Additionally, accounts with incomplete or outdated Know Your Customer (KYC) information will also be at risk of closure. To avoid this, account holders must ensure their KYC updates are current and accurate.
