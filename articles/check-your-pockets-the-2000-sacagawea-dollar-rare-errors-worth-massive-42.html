Check Your Pockets: The 2000 Sacagawea Dollar Rare Errors Worth Massive Money Today

By James Carter Senior Editor
Published: May 06, 2026 Updated: May 06, 2026 7 Min Read

The year 2000 saw the introduction of the Sacagawea dollar, a gold-colored coin minted to honor the Shoshone woman who played a crucial role in the Lewis and Clark expedition. Among the millions of coins produced, certain rare error coins have emerged, making them highly valuable to collectors. Estimates suggest that some of these error coins can sell for significantly high prices, reportedly ranging from five-figure to six-figure sums.

What makes the 2000 Sacagawea dollar special is the unique combination of its historical significance, limited production, and the presence of rare error coins. The coin's design, featuring Sacagawea carrying her infant son Jean Baptiste, was a significant departure from traditional coin designs. As a result, the 2000 Sacagawea dollar has become a highly sought-after collectible, with rare error coins being particularly prized by collectors.

How to Spot a Rare Coin in Your Pocket

Spotting a rare coin in your pocket requires a combination of knowledge, attention to detail, and luck. One of the most important things to look for is the coin's condition, with rare coins often being in excellent condition. You should also examine the coin's design, looking for any errors or defects that could indicate a rare coin.

Another important factor to consider is the coin's serial number, with unique or low serial numbers often being highly valuable. You should also look for any signs of wear or damage, as rare coins are often well-preserved. By combining these factors, you can increase your chances of spotting a rare coin in your pocket.
