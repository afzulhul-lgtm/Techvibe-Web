Check Your Pockets: The 2000 Sacagawea Dollar Rare Errors Worth Massive Money Today

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: April 27, 2026 Updated: April 27, 2026 8 Min Read

The year 2000 Sacagawea dollar is a highly sought-after coin among collectors, and for good reason. This coin, which was minted to commemorate the Lewis and Clark expedition, features a unique design and a rich history. However, what makes some of these coins truly special is the presence of rare errors that can significantly increase their value. If you're like many people, you may have a few of these coins lying around in your pocket change or stored away in a jar. But before you consider them to be just ordinary coins, it's worth taking a closer look to see if you might have stumbled upon a rare error.

The Sacagawea dollar was first introduced in 2000 as part of a new dollar coin program aimed at increasing the circulation of dollar coins. The coin features a portrait of Sacagawea, the Shoshone woman who played a crucial role in the Lewis and Clark expedition, on the obverse side, and an image of an eagle on the reverse side. While the coin was designed to be a circulating coin, many people chose to save them as collectibles, which has helped to drive up demand and value over time. Now, with the rise of coin collecting and the increased interest in rare error coins, the value of certain Sacagawea dollars has skyrocketed.

Understanding Rare Error Coins

Rare error coins are coins that have been minted with mistakes or irregularities, such as misprinted dates, misaligned dies, or incorrect metal compositions. These errors can occur during the minting process, and they can significantly increase the value of a coin. In the case of the Sacagawea dollar, there are several rare error coins that are highly prized among collectors. One of the most notable errors is the "cheerio" error, which features a misprinted edge lettering. Other errors include the "mule" error, which features a Sacagawea obverse paired with a reverse from a different coin, and the "double-struck" error, which features a coin that has been struck twice.

These rare error coins are highly sought after by collectors, and they can command high prices at auction. However, it's worth noting that the value of a rare error coin depends on several factors, including its rarity, condition, and demand. If you think you might have a rare error coin, it's essential to have it authenticated and graded by a reputable third-party service, such as the Professional Coin Grading Service (PCGS) or the Numismatic Guaranty Corporation (NGC). This will help to verify the coin's authenticity and determine its value.

In addition to their monetary value, rare error coins also offer a unique glimpse into the minting process and the history of coin production. By studying these errors, collectors and numismatists can gain a better understanding of how coins are made and the various factors that can affect their production. This knowledge can also help to inform the development of new coins and the improvement of minting techniques.

Identifying Rare Error Coins

Identifying rare error coins requires a keen eye for detail and a thorough understanding of the minting process. One of the best ways to start is by examining the coin's edge lettering, which can reveal misprinted or misaligned letters. You should also look for any irregularities in the coin's design, such as misprinted dates or incorrect metal compositions. Additionally, you can use a magnifying glass or a coin loupe to examine the coin's surface for any signs of double striking or other minting errors.

Another helpful tool for identifying rare error coins is a coin catalog or price guide. These resources can provide valuable information about the different types of errors that can occur on a particular coin, as well as their relative rarity and value. You can also join a coin collecting community or online forum, where you can connect with other collectors and learn from their experiences.

When examining a coin for rare errors, it's essential to be patient and thorough. You should also be aware of the potential for counterfeit coins, which can be difficult to distinguish from genuine error coins. To avoid falling victim to counterfeiting, make sure to purchase coins from reputable dealers and have them authenticated and graded by a third-party service.

The Value of Rare Error Coins

The value of rare error coins can vary widely, depending on their rarity, condition, and demand. Some of the most valuable error coins can sell for tens of thousands of dollars at auction, while others may be worth only a few hundred dollars. However, even if a rare error coin is not worth a fortune, it can still be a valuable addition to a coin collection.

In addition to their monetary value, rare error coins can also offer a unique sense of history and nostalgia. By collecting and studying these coins, you can gain a deeper appreciation for the minting process and the people who have shaped the history of coin production. You can also learn about the different design elements and security features that are used to prevent counterfeiting and ensure the integrity of the coin supply.
