Introduction to the 2000 Sacagawea Dollar

The 2000 Sacagawea dollar was minted to commemorate the historic expedition of Lewis and Clark, led by Sacagawea, a Shoshone woman who played a crucial role in the journey. The coin features a unique design, with Sacagawea and her infant son, Jean Baptiste, on the obverse side, and an eagle on the reverse side. The coin was minted in 2000 and has since become a highly sought-after collector's item.

Identifying Rare Errors

Identifying rare errors requires a keen eye for detail and a thorough understanding of the coin's design and minting process. Some common errors to look out for include misprinted dates, misaligned dies, and unusual edge markings. By studying the coin carefully and researching its history, you can increase your chances of finding a rare error.
