China and Portugal Combine: Why Macao is One of 2026’s Most Unique City Breaks

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: April 08, 2026 Updated: April 08, 2026 7 Min Read

Imagine a city where Chinese temples and Portuguese colonial architecture coexist, where the sound of Cantonese and Portuguese fills the air, and where the smell of dim sum and pastries wafts through the streets. Welcome to Macao, a unique city break destination that combines the best of China and Portugal. With over 30 million visitors expected in 2026, Macao is poised to become one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world.

Macao's unique blend of cultures is a result of its 400-year history as a Portuguese colony. The city's historic center is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring over 20 historic buildings, including the famous Ruins of St. Paul's and the Senado Square. Visitors can explore the city's museums, temples, and markets, sampling the local cuisine and drinks, such as egg tarts and Port wine.

What Makes Macao a Unique City Break?

Macao's unique blend of cultures is just one of the many reasons why it's a must-visit destination in 2026. The city offers a wide range of entertainment options, including casinos, shows, and nightlife. Visitors can try their luck at the City of Dreams or the Venetian Macao, or catch a performance at the Macau Tower. For those looking for something more adventurous, Macao offers a range of outdoor activities, such as hiking and rock climbing.

One of the best ways to experience Macao's unique culture is to try the local cuisine. The city is famous for its fusion food, which combines Chinese and Portuguese flavors. Visitors can sample dim sum at a traditional tea house, or try some Portuguese-style barbecue at a local restaurant. For those with a sweet tooth, Macao's bakeries offer a wide range of pastries and cakes, including the famous egg tart.

Must-Try Foods in Macao

Some of the must-try foods in Macao include minchi, a traditional Macanese dish made with minced meat and onions, and feijoada, a Portuguese-style bean stew. Visitors can also try some fresh seafood at one of the city's many restaurants, or sample some local street food at a night market.

How to Plan Your Trip to Macao

Planning a trip to Macao is relatively easy, with many airlines offering direct flights to the city. Visitors can also take a ferry from Hong Kong or Guangzhou. Once in Macao, visitors can get around the city using taxis or buses, or rent a car for the day. For those looking for a more convenient option, many hotels offer shuttle services to and from the airport.

One of the best ways to experience Macao is to stay in a hotel that offers a range of amenities and services. Some of the top hotels in Macao include the Four Seasons Hotel Macao, the Mandarin Oriental Macau, and the Wynn Macau. Visitors can also stay in a hostel or guesthouse for a more budget-friendly option.

What to Expect from Macao's Entertainment Scene

Macao's entertainment scene is one of the most vibrant and diverse in the world. Visitors can choose from a range of shows, including acrobatic performances, concerts, and theater productions. The city is also home to a number of casinos, including the City of Dreams and the Venetian Macao. For those looking for something more low-key, Macao's bars and clubs offer a range of drinks and music.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Macao?

The best time to visit Macao is from October to December, when the weather is cool and dry. Visitors can enjoy the city's outdoor activities, such as and rock climbing, and take in the festivals and events that take place during this time.

What is the currency used in Macao?

The currency used in Macao is the Macanese pataca, although Hong Kong dollars and Chinese yuan are also widely accepted. Visitors can exchange their money at a bank or currency exchange, or use an ATM to withdraw cash.

Is Macao a safe city to visit?

Yes, Macao is a safe city to visit, with low crime rates compared to other major cities. Visitors should still take the usual precautions to protect themselves and their belongings, such as being aware of their surroundings and keeping valuables secure.

What is the language spoken in Macao?

The official languages spoken in Macao are Cantonese and Portuguese, although Mandarin and English are also widely spoken. Visitors can get by with English, as many signs and menus are translated, and many locals speak some English.

How do I get to Macao?

Visitors can get to Macao by air, with many airlines offering direct flights to the city. Alternatively, visitors can take a ferry from Hong Kong or Guangzhou, or take a bus or taxi from the mainland.
