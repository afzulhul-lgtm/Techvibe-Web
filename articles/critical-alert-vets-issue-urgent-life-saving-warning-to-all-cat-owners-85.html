The American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Poison Control Center reports that lilies, including Easter lilies, tiger lilies, and day lilies, are among the most deadly plants for cats. But why does this matter? It's hard to say exactly why these plants are so toxic, but what's clear is that even a small amount of ingestion can cause acute kidney failure and death within 72 hours if left untreated.

That's where it gets interesting. The warning isn't just about plants; it also includes xylitol, an artificial sweetener found in sugar-free gums, some peanut butters, and dental products. While primarily toxic to dogs, xylitol can also affect cats.
