Expected Snowfall Totals and Dangerous Conditions

The expected snowfall totals are significant, with 8 to 18 inches of snow expected across the primary impact zone. The heavy wet snow, combined with near-zero visibility in heavy snowbands and plunging overnight temperatures, will create rapid icing of road surfaces, making travel conditions extremely hazardous. Even four-wheel-drive vehicles will provide no protection against black ice forming beneath fresh snow.

Power outages are also a concern, with heavy wet snow loads accumulating on transmission lines and tree branches. Residents are advised to charge devices, fill vehicle fuel tanks before the storm intensifies, and have emergency food and water for at least 72 hours. Emergency shelters have been activated in multiple jurisdictions for those without adequate heating.

How to Prepare Your Vehicle and Home

To prepare for the winter storm, residents are advised to charge devices, fill vehicle fuel tanks, and have emergency food and water for at least 72 hours. It is also essential to keep prescription medications accessible and to have a plan in place in case of a power outage. Residents are urged to stay indoors and avoid travel unless absolutely necessary.

In addition to preparing their homes, residents should also take steps to prepare their vehicles. This includes filling up on gas, checking tire pressure, and ensuring that all necessary safety equipment, such as flashlights and first aid kits, are in the vehicle.

When Conditions Are Expected to Improve

Conditions are expected to improve after the storm passes, with temperatures rising and snowfall rates decreasing. However, the greatest danger window spans from this evening through early morning tomorrow, when temperatures are expected to drop sharply and wind gusts may compound visibility and road conditions. Residents are advised to stay indoors and avoid travel during this time.

Once the storm has passed, residents can expect road crews to work on clearing roads and restoring power to affected areas. It is essential to exercise caution when traveling, even after the storm has passed, as roads may still be hazardous and power outages may persist.
