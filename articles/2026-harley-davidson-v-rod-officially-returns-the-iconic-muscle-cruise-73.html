2026 Harley-Davidson V-Rod Officially Returns: The Iconic Muscle Cruiser's Shocking Specs & Price

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Editor
Published: May 10, 2026 Updated: May 10, 2026 6 Min Read

The 2026 Harley-Davidson V-Rod is officially back, and it's making waves in the motorcycle community. The iconic muscle cruiser, which was discontinued in 2017, is returning with a vengeance, and its specs and price are turning heads. From what's been reported so far, the new V-Rod is expected to feature a larger displacement of 1,250cc or 1,300cc, producing 135 to 145 horsepower, potentially with mild-hybrid assistance.

But why does this matter? The V-Rod's return is significant because it marks a shift in Harley-Davidson's strategy to cater to a younger, more performance-oriented demographic. The company has been focusing on its touring lineup in recent years, but the V-Rod's revival suggests that they're looking to diversify their offerings and appeal to a broader range of riders. That's not entirely clear yet, but based on available information, it's likely that the new V-Rod will meet Euro 5 and US EPA emissions standards without sacrificing performance.

What the Forecast Shows

According to sources, the 2026 Harley-Davidson V-Rod will be powered by a liquid-cooled V-twin engine, similar to the original Revolution engine that produced 115 horsepower. The new engine is expected to be more powerful, with some reports suggesting that it could produce up to 145 horsepower. It's hard to say exactly why Harley-Davidson is opting for a more powerful engine, but it's likely that they're looking to compete with other high-performance motorcycles on the market.

Worth noting is that the V-Rod's return is not just about the specs and price; it's also about the brand's reputation and appeal. Harley-Davidson has been struggling to attract younger riders in recent years, and the V-Rod's revival could be a major opportunity for them to reconnect with this demographic. Yet, it's unclear how the market will respond to the new V-Rod, and whether it will be enough to boost Harley-Davidson's sales and revenue.

Which Areas Are Affected

The 2026 Harley-Davidson V-Rod's specs and price will likely have a significant impact on the motorcycle market, particularly in the high-performance segment. The V-Rod's return could potentially disrupt the sales of other motorcycles in this segment, including those from competitors like Honda. To put that in perspective, the V-Rod's original price range was around $17,000 to $22,000, which is comparable to the current prices of Harley-Davidson's Sport Glide and Nightster models.

But what that means, practically speaking, is that Harley-Davidson will need to ensure that the new V-Rod meets the expectations of its target audience, which is a challenging task given the intense competition in the motorcycle market.
