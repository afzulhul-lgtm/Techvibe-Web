Eclipse of the century: nearly six full minutes of darkness when it will happen and the best places to watch mapped out

By Sarah Mitchell
23 Feb, 2026

The world is eagerly waiting for the Total solar eclipse 2027, an event that will bring darkness in the middle of the day. This rare celestial event will occur when the moon passes directly between the Earth and the sun, blocking the sun's light. The Total solar eclipse 2027 will be a unique experience, with nearly six full minutes of darkness, making it a must-see event for astronomy enthusiasts and the general public alike.

What to Expect

The Total solar eclipse 2027 will be visible from certain parts of the world, including North America, Europe, and Asia. The path of totality, where the eclipse will be visible in its entirety, will stretch across several countries, offering a rare opportunity to witness this phenomenon. The eclipse will occur on a specific date, allowing people to plan ahead and make necessary arrangements to witness this event.

Best Viewing Spots

To witness the Total solar eclipse 2027, people will need to be in the path of totality. Some of the best viewing spots will include national parks, observatories, and other locations with minimal light pollution. These spots will offer a clear view of the eclipse, allowing people to fully experience the beauty of this rare event. The best viewing spots will be crowded, so it's essential to plan ahead and book accommodations and viewing spots in advance.
