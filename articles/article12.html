Understanding the Polar Vortex

The polar vortex is a natural weather pattern that forms over the Arctic region during the winter months. However, this year's vortex has been particularly strong, with temperatures plummeting to record lows in many areas. The arctic blast has been so severe that it's caused widespread disruptions to daily life, from cancelled flights to frozen pipes.

What's Driving the Extreme Weather?

So, what's behind this extreme weather shift? Scientists point to climate change as a major factor, with rising global temperatures causing the polar vortex to become more unstable. This instability leads to a rare weather phenomenon known as a "sudden stratospheric warming" event, which can have far-reaching consequences for our weather patterns.
