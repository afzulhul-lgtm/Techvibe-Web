The Background You Need to Know

To understand why broccoli, cabbage, and cauliflower are the same plant, it's necessary to delve into the world of plant classification. The system of categorizing plants into different species and genera is based on a combination of physical characteristics, such as the shape and color of the leaves, and genetic data.

In the case of broccoli, cabbage, and cauliflower, these vegetables are all part of the Brassica genus, which includes a wide range of plant species. Within this genus, there are several different species, including Brassica oleracea, which is the species to which broccoli, cabbage, and cauliflower all belong.
