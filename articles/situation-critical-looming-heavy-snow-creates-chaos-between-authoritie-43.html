The contradiction between authorities and businesses has placed workers, customers, and emergency responders in danger, with traffic volumes remaining dangerously high during the storm. What the Weather Warning Says

The storm has already had a significant impact on the region, with snow plow operators reporting being unable to make meaningful progress on arterial roads due to the volume of civilian traffic. Hospital emergency departments in the impact zone have activated mass casualty protocols as accident volumes exceed normal capacity.

State emergency operations centers are fielding calls from businesses seeking clarification on the legal obligations and liability exposure they face if employees are injured during mandatory shifts in dangerous conditions. Employment attorneys note that the question cuts both ways, with employers maintaining operations during declared emergencies potentially facing premises liability and negligence claims if workers are injured in storm-related accidents commuting to or from work.

The Conflict Between Authorities and Businesses

The lack of explicit legal closure mandates in most jurisdictions means that many businesses calculate that the commercial risk of closure outweighs the liability risk of remaining open. This has led to a standoff between authorities and businesses, with the former demanding closures and the latter choosing to remain operational.

Authorities are now considering escalating from advisory to mandatory travel ban status, a step that carries enforcement authority but significant public pushback risk. The decision to implement a mandatory travel ban would have significant implications for businesses and residents in the affected area.
