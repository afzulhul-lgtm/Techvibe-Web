Under the Hood

The 2026 Maserati Quattroporte is equipped with a potent 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 engine, producing 430 horsepower and 428 lb-ft of torque. This powertrain allows the Quattroporte to go from 0-60 mph in just 4.7 seconds, making it a formidable competitor in the luxury sedan segment.

But that's not the whole picture. The Quattroporte's engine is also paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission and a rear-biased all-wheel-drive system, providing exceptional handling and control. Whether navigating twisty roads or cruising down the highway, the Quattroporte's advanced powertrain ensures a smooth and engaging driving experience.

The numbers tell a different story when it comes to fuel efficiency, however. With an estimated 17 mpg combined, the Quattroporte lags behind some of its competitors in terms of fuel economy. Nonetheless, for those who prioritize performance and style, this may be a trade-off worth considering.

Design and Features

Inside and out, the 2026 Maserati Quattroporte exudes elegance and sophistication. Its sleek, aerodynamic design is accentuated by a prominent grille, sweeping lines, and a distinctive rear spoiler. The interior is equally impressive, with premium leather upholstery, heated and ventilated seats, and a state-of-the-art infotainment system.

What changed for the 2026 model year is the introduction of several new features, including a redesigned dashboard, an updated navigation system, and a suite of advanced safety features. These enhancements not only improve the overall driving experience but also provide added peace of mind for passengers.

Here's the thing: while the Quattroporte's design and features are certainly impressive, they come at a cost. With a starting price of over $100,000, this luxury sedan is undoubtedly an investment. But for those who value the finer things in life, the Quattroporte's unique blend of style, performance, and luxury may be well worth the expense.

Performance and Handling

The 2026 Maserati Quattroporte is more than just a pretty face - it's also a capable performer. With its powerful engine and advanced all-wheel-drive system, this luxury sedan can tackle even the most challenging roads with ease. Whether carving through tight corners or accelerating down a straightaway, the Quattroporte's responsive handling and precise steering make it a joy to drive.

That's where it gets complicated, however. While the Quattroporte's performance is undeniably impressive, it's not without its drawbacks. The vehicle's large size and weight can make it feel less agile than some of its competitors, and the ride can be somewhat firm at times. Nonetheless, for those who prioritize power and style, these trade-offs may be well worth it.
