2026 Maserati Quattroporte Review: 430 HP Luxury Sedan Specs & Price

By {{AUTHOR_NAME}} Senior Editor
Published: May 08, 2026 Updated: May 08, 2026 6 Min Read

The 2026 Maserati Quattroporte accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in approximately 4.5 seconds, thanks to its smooth, Ferrari-derived V6 engine delivering 430 horsepower. This propulsion system enables the sedan to reach a top speed of 177 mph, making it one of the fastest luxury saloons in its class. With its standard eight-speed automatic gearbox, the Quattroporte handles power delivery with reasonable composure, although some purists note that the shifts lack the seamlessness of its rivals.

The current market demand for luxury sedans with unique character and driver engagement makes the 2026 Quattroporte a notable entry. As Maserati's last great naturally aspirated model before full electrification, the 2026 edition is especially collectible. With its distinctive Italian design, the Quattroporte sets itself apart from German rivals like the BMW 7 Series and Mercedes S-Class.

What the 2026 Quattroporte Offers at First Glance

The Quattroporte's exterior features Maserati's signature proportions, including a long bonnet, sculpted flanks, and the iconic trident badge on the front grille. The boomerang-shaped LED rear lights pay homage to the legendary 3200 GT. Inside, the cabin blends genuine Italian craftsmanship with Harman Kardon premium audio as standard, while the optional Bowers and Wilkins surround sound system elevates the experience further.

The 10.1-inch MIA infotainment touchscreen runs on Android Automotive OS with Amazon Alexa voice control built in, providing a modern and intuitive interface. Rear passengers enjoy generous legroom in the 5.3-meter long body, with the GranLusso trim adding opulent rear recliners and ventilated seats for enhanced comfort.

Engine Power and Performance Breakdown

The Quattroporte's powertrain is a key aspect of its appeal, with the range-topping Trofeo variant upgrading to a Ferrari-sourced 3.8-litre V8 producing 580 horsepower. This significant boost in power makes the Trofeo a compelling option for those seeking exceptional performance. In contrast, the standard V6 engine still delivers impressive acceleration and a respectable fuel economy of around 28 mpg on combined roads.

The Quattroporte's performance capabilities make it an attractive choice for drivers who value speed and agility. With its smooth engine and responsive handling, the Quattroporte provides a driving experience that is both exhilarating and refined.

Interior Luxury and Technology Features

The Quattroporte's interior is a showcase of Italian craftsmanship, with premium materials and attention to detail evident throughout. The standard Harman Kardon audio system provides excellent sound quality, while the optional Bowers and Wilkins surround sound system offers an even more immersive experience. The 10.1-inch MIA infotainment touchscreen is intuitive and easy to use, with Amazon Alexa voice control providing convenient access to various functions.

The Quattroporte's luxury features extend to its seats, with the GranLusso trim offering opulent rear recliners and ventilated seats. Rear passengers also enjoy generous legroom, making the Quattroporte an excellent choice for those who value comfort and space.

Key Takeaways

  • The 2026 Maserati Quattroporte has a 430 horsepower V6 engine and reaches a top speed of 177 mph.
  • The Quattroporte features a standard eight-speed automatic gearbox and has a fuel economy of around 28 mpg on combined roads.
  • The interior includes premium materials, a 10.1-inch MIA infotainment touchscreen, and a Harman Kardon audio system as standard.

How It Compares to German Rivals

The Quattroporte's unique blend of Italian style and performance sets it apart from German rivals like the BMW 7 Series and Mercedes S-Class. While these models may offer more technology and features, the Quattroporte's focus on driver engagement and emotional character makes it a compelling choice for those who value a more authentic driving experience.

In terms of performance, the Quattroporte's 430 horsepower V6 engine and smooth handling make it a strong contender in its class. With its luxurious interior and advanced technology features, the Quattroporte is an attractive option for those seeking a unique and refined driving experience.

Price Range and Final Verdict

The 2026 Maserati Quattroporte's base pricing starts in the high $80,000s, climbing to over $200,000 for the Trofeo variant. This pricing reflects the Quattroporte's luxurious features, advanced technology, and exceptional performance capabilities. As Maserati's last great naturally aspirated model, the 2026 Quattroporte is a significant entry in the luxury sedan market.

With its unique blend of Italian style, performance, and luxury, the 2026 Quattroporte is an excellent choice for those seeking a refined and exhilarating driving experience. As the market continues to evolve, the Quattroporte's focus on driver engagement and emotional character makes it a compelling option for those who value authenticity and craftsmanship.
