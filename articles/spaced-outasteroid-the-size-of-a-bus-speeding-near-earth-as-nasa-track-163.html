SPACED OUTAsteroid the size of a bus speeding near Earth as NASA tracks chilling encounter

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: April 28, 2026 Updated: April 28, 2026 7 Min Read

The vastness of space is filled with mysterious objects, including asteroids that are remnants from the early days of our solar system. These small, rocky bodies are often found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, but some have strayed from their orbits and are now hurtling through space. Recently, an asteroid the size of a bus was tracked by NASA as it sped near Earth, providing a chilling encounter that highlights the importance of monitoring these celestial bodies.

The asteroid, which was estimated to be around 10 meters in diameter, was detected by NASA's asteroid detection system, which uses a combination of ground-based telescopes and space-based observatories to track near-Earth objects. The system is designed to provide early warnings of potential asteroid impacts, allowing scientists to study the asteroid's trajectory and determine if it poses a threat to our planet. In this case, the asteroid was found to be passing safely by Earth, but its close approach highlights the need for continued monitoring and tracking of these objects.

Understanding Asteroids and Their Orbits

Asteroids are small, rocky objects that orbit the Sun, and they can be found in various locations throughout our solar system. The majority of asteroids are located in the asteroid belt, a region between Mars and Jupiter where the gravitational pull of the two planets helps to keep the asteroids in stable orbits. However, some asteroids have strayed from their orbits and are now found in other regions of the solar system, including near Earth. These near-Earth asteroids, or NEAs, are of particular interest to scientists because they can provide insights into the early formation and evolution of our solar system.

The orbits of asteroids can be affected by various factors, including the gravitational pull of nearby planets and the Yarkovsky effect, a phenomenon in which the Sun's radiation causes an asteroid to experience a slight force that can alter its orbit over time. As a result, some asteroids can have highly elliptical orbits that bring them close to the Sun and then out to the outer reaches of the solar system. Understanding the orbits of asteroids is crucial for predicting their trajectories and determining if they pose a threat to Earth.

The study of asteroids has also provided valuable insights into the early formation and evolution of our solar system. By analyzing the composition and structure of asteroids, scientists can gain a better understanding of the materials that were present in the early solar system and how they were processed and transformed over time. This knowledge can help scientists to better understand the formation of the planets and the development of life on Earth.

NASA's Asteroid Detection and Tracking System

NASA's asteroid detection and tracking system is a critical component of the agency's efforts to identify and monitor near-Earth asteroids. The system uses a combination of ground-based telescopes and space-based observatories to detect and track asteroids, providing early warnings of potential asteroid impacts. The system is designed to detect asteroids that are at least 140 meters in diameter, which is the size of an asteroid that could cause significant damage if it were to impact Earth.

The system uses a variety of techniques to detect and track asteroids, including optical telescopes, radar, and infrared sensors. The Catalina Sky Survey, a ground-based telescope system, is one of the most productive asteroid detection systems in the world, having discovered thousands of asteroids since its inception. The NEOWISE mission, a space-based telescope, has also been used to detect and track asteroids, providing valuable insights into their composition and structure.

The data provided by these systems is used to predict the trajectories of asteroids and determine if they pose a threat to Earth. If an asteroid is found to be on a collision course with our planet, scientists can use this information to develop strategies for deflecting or disrupting the asteroid, preventing a potential impact.

The Importance of Asteroid Research and Monitoring

Asteroid research and monitoring are essential for understanding the potential risks and benefits associated with these celestial bodies. By studying asteroids, scientists can gain a better understanding of the early formation and evolution of our solar system, as well as the potential resources that asteroids may provide. However, asteroids can also pose a threat to Earth, and monitoring their orbits and trajectories is critical for predicting and preventing potential impacts.

The study of asteroids has also led to the development of new technologies and strategies for asteroid deflection and disruption. For example, NASA's DART mission, which is scheduled to launch in 2022, will test a technique for deflecting an asteroid by colliding a spacecraft with it. This technology could potentially be used to prevent a large asteroid from impacting Earth in the future.
