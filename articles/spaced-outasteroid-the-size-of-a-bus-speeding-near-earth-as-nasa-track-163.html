By Dr. Amara Singh Senior Editor
Published: May 06, 2026 Updated: May 06, 2026 9 Min Read

The discovery of near-Earth asteroids, such as the recently tracked bus-sized asteroid, highlights the importance of asteroid detection and tracking systems. Asteroids like 101955 Bennu, a carbonaceous asteroid approximately 500 meters in diameter, have been the focus of NASA's OSIRIS-REx mission, which aimed to study the asteroid's composition and return samples to Earth. The asteroid 101955 Bennu is of particular interest due to its proximity to Earth and potential for future impacts.

The study of asteroids like 101955 Bennu and the recently tracked bus-sized asteroid is crucial for understanding the risks and consequences of asteroid impacts. The Chicxulub asteroid impact, which occurred during the Cretaceous-Paleogene boundary approximately 66 million years ago, is a prime example of the devastating effects of asteroid impacts. This impact, which is believed to have contributed to the extinction of the dinosaurs, highlights the importance of detecting and tracking near-Earth asteroids.

Scientists have discovered that asteroids like 101955 Bennu have a highly elliptical orbit, which brings them close to Earth's orbit. The asteroid 101955 Bennu is estimated to have a 1 in 2,700 chance of impacting Earth between 2175 and 2196. The recently tracked bus-sized asteroid, although not as large as 101955 Bennu, still poses a significant threat due to its size and proximity to Earth. Researchers use various methods, including radar and optical telescopes, to detect and track near-Earth asteroids.

The Arecibo Observatory in Puerto Rico, for example, uses radar to detect and track near-Earth asteroids. The observatory's 305-meter dish antenna can detect asteroids as small as 10 meters in diameter. The Catalina Sky Survey, a NASA-funded project, uses optical telescopes to detect near-Earth asteroids. The survey has discovered thousands of near-Earth asteroids, including several potentially hazardous ones.

The detection and tracking of near-Earth asteroids are critical for preventing potential impacts. NASA's Planetary Defense Coordination Office (PDCO) is responsible for detecting and tracking near-Earth asteroids. The PDCO uses a combination of ground-based and space-based telescopes to detect and track asteroids. The office also collaborates with international partners to share data and coordinate response efforts.
