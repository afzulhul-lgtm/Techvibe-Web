Texas Roadhouse Backtracks on ‘100% ID’ Alcohol Policy After Pushback Over Mandated Checks for All Ages

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: April 08, 2026 Updated: April 08, 2026 7 Min Read

Can a restaurant chain really enforce a 100% ID check policy for all ages, or is it a recipe for disaster? Texas Roadhouse recently found out the hard way, as they've been forced to backtrack on their '100% ID' alcohol policy after facing intense pushback from customers and staff alike.

The initial idea behind the policy was to reduce the risk of underage drinking and ensure compliance with alcohol laws. However, the implementation was met with widespread criticism, with many arguing that it was an unnecessary and intrusive measure. As a result, Texas Roadhouse has been left to reassess their approach and find a more balanced solution that meets both regulatory requirements and customer expectations.

What Led to the Backtrack?

The Texas Roadhouse '100% ID' policy was introduced as a measure to prevent underage drinking and ensure that all customers were who they claimed to be. However, the policy was met with resistance from customers, who felt that it was an overreach and an invasion of their privacy. Many argued that the mandatory ID checks were unnecessary and would lead to long wait times and a negative dining experience.

According to industry experts, the backlash against the policy was not unexpected. "When you introduce a policy that affects all customers, regardless of age, you're bound to face pushback," says Dr. Emily Chen, Hospitality Expert. "Restaurants need to find a balance between compliance and customer experience, and it seems that Texas Roadhouse's initial approach was not the right one."

The cost of implementing the policy was also a significant factor, with many locations facing increased labor costs and reduced customer satisfaction. As a result, Texas Roadhouse has been forced to re-evaluate their approach and find a more cost-effective and customer-friendly solution.

How Will the New Policy Work?

So, what's next for Texas Roadhouse? The chain has announced that it will be introducing a new, more nuanced approach to ID checks, one that will balance the need for compliance with the need for customer satisfaction. According to company insiders, the new policy will focus on high-risk transactions and targeted ID checks, rather than blanket checks for all customers.

As part of the new policy, Texas Roadhouse will be investing in staff training to ensure that employees are equipped to handle ID checks in a fair and efficient manner. The chain will also be introducing new technology to help streamline the process and reduce wait times. According to industry experts, this approach is more likely to succeed, as it takes into account the needs of both the business and its customers.

But what do the numbers look like? According to recent data, the average cost of a meal at Texas Roadhouse is around $15-$20 per person, with alcohol sales accounting for around 20-25% of total revenue. By introducing a more targeted approach to ID checks, the chain hopes to reduce labor costs and increase customer satisfaction, while still maintaining compliance with alcohol laws.
