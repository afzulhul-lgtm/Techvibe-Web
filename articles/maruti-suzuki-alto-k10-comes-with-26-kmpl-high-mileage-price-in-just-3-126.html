Maruti Suzuki Alto K10 comes with 26 kmpl high mileage, price in just ₹3 Lakhs

By Sarah Mitchell
Published: March 04, 2026 Updated: March 04, 2026 5 Min Read

As we roll into 2026, the Maruti Suzuki Alto K10 refuses to fade away, holding strong with fresh offers and tweaks that keep it relevant in a market flooded with flashy SUVs. The Alto K10 has been the quiet hero for millions of Indians facing bumper-to-bumper traffic, cursing the fuel gauge that’s dipping faster than their weekend plans.

The Maruti Suzuki Alto K10 comes with 26 kmpl high mileage, price in just ₹3 Lakhs, making it an attractive option for first-time buyers, fleets, or backup cars. With its compact design, peppy engine, and affordable price, the Alto K10 is a reliable choice for city driving.

Key Takeaways

  • Maruti Suzuki Alto K10 comes with 26 kmpl high mileage
  • Price starts at ₹3 Lakhs
  • Compact design with a length of 3530mm
  • 1.0-litre K10C three-cylinder petrol engine
  • Manual five-speed gearbox and AMT automatic options

A Timeless Design with Subtle 2026 Polish

The 2026 model sticks to the script: a compact 3530mm length that slips into the tightest parking spots in Delhi or Mumbai like it owns them. Those halogen headlights got a minor glow-up last year, paired with a honeycomb grille that gives it just enough modern edge without screaming for attention.

Features and Comfort

Slide into the cabin, and it’s like stepping into a no-nonsense friend’s living room—functional, uncluttered, and surprisingly roomy for five. Front seats are decently cushioned, with headroom that tall folks appreciate, thanks to the high roofline.

Power That Punches Above Its Weight

Pop the hood, and there’s the familiar 1.0-litre K10C three-cylinder petrol engine churning out 66.65 bhp at 5500 rpm and 89 Nm of torque. It’s peppy enough for city sprints, hitting 100 kmph without feeling strained, though highways over 120 kmph remind you it’s no racetrack special.

Safety: Better Than Before, But Room to Grow

Maruti finally listened—ESP is now standard across the board since 2024, alongside dual airbags, ABS with EBD, and rear parking sensors. It earned a 2-star Global NCAP back in 2023, but reinforced body and three-point belts for all help in real crashes.

Pricing and Variants: Wallet-Friendly Choices

Starting at ₹3.70 lakh ex-showroom for the base STD (O), it climbs to ₹5.45 lakh for the loaded VXi+ (O) AMT. On-road in Delhi? Expect ₹4.2-6.1 lakh, with eight variants meaning options: LXi for basics, VXi for features, CNG for mileage chasers.
