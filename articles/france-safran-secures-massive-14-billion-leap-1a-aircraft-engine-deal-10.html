France Safran Secures Massive €1.4 Billion LEAP-1A Aircraft Engine Deal

By {{AUTHOR_NAME}} Senior Editor
Published: May 08, 2026 Updated: May 08, 2026 6 Min Read

The recent announcement of France's Safran securing a massive €1.4 billion deal for its LEAP-1A aircraft engines marks a significant milestone in the company's history. This contract, which was announced at the Dubai Airshow in November 2025, covers 120 LEAP-1A engines destined for Riyadh Air, the new Saudi Arabian carrier launched under the country's Vision 2030 strategy.

This deal matters now because it not only reinforces Safran's status as an industrial flagship but also underscores the growing importance of the Middle East in the global aviation market. With Riyadh Air aiming to transform the Saudi capital into a major global aviation hub competing with Dubai and Doha, this contract is a testament to the region's increasing influence in the industry.

What the €1.4 Billion LEAP-1A Deal Covers

The €1.4 billion deal covers the supply of 120 LEAP-1A engines, which will power 60 Airbus A321neo aircraft for Riyadh Air. Based on industry pricing of approximately €12 million per LEAP-1A engine, the hardware alone represents roughly €1.4 billion. However, the total lifetime value of the contract will climb significantly once spare parts, maintenance contracts, and technical support are factored in over the engines' 20 to 25-year operational lifespan.

The LEAP-1A engines will be assembled at Safran's plants in Villaroche and Saint-Quentin, securing skilled jobs on both sides of the Atlantic. The deal also includes the latest high-pressure turbine durability kit, specifically engineered to withstand the harsh desert conditions of Saudi Arabia.

Who Is Riyadh Air and Why This Deal Matters

Riyadh Air is the new Saudi Arabian carrier launched under the country's Vision 2030 strategy, which aims to transform the Saudi capital into a major global aviation hub. This deal is significant for Riyadh Air as it marks a major step towards building its fleet from scratch. With ambitions to compete with Dubai and Doha, Riyadh Air's decision to opt for the LEAP-1A engine is a vote of confidence in the engine's performance and efficiency.

The LEAP-1A engine, which stands for Leading Edge Aviation Propulsion, has already become one of the best-selling jet engines in history. Over 4,000 units have already been delivered, with a backlog of approximately 10,000 engines still on order. This deal further cements the engine's position in the market and underscores its popularity among airlines.

The LEAP-1A Engine — Technical Breakdown

The LEAP-1A engine delivers a 15 percent improvement in fuel efficiency and a corresponding reduction in CO2 emissions compared to the previous CFM56 generation. Additionally, the engine features meaningfully reduced noise levels, which is a growing priority for airports worldwide. The engine's advanced technology and design make it an attractive option for airlines looking to reduce their environmental footprint while improving their bottom line.

The LEAP-1A engine's technical specifications are a testament to the engineering prowess of Safran and its joint venture partner, GE Aerospace. With a thrust range of 24,000 to 35,000 pounds, the engine is capable of powering a wide range of aircraft, from the Airbus A319neo to the A321neo.

How This Strengthens France's Aerospace Position

The €1.4 billion deal is a significant boost to France's aerospace industry, reinforcing Safran's status as an industrial flagship. The company's ability to secure such a large contract is a testament to its engineering expertise and its position as a leading player in the global aerospace market.

The deal also underscores the importance of the aerospace industry to France's economy. With thousands of skilled jobs secured at Safran's plants in Villaroche and Saint-Quentin, the contract is a significant contributor to the country's economic growth and development.

What Happens Next for Safran and CFM International

With the €1.4 billion deal secured, Safran and its joint venture partner, GE Aerospace, will focus on delivering the 120 LEAP-1A engines to Riyadh Air. The companies will also continue to work on developing new technologies and improving the engine's performance and efficiency.

The deal is expected to have a positive impact on Safran's financial performance, with the company forecasting significant revenue growth in the coming years. As the aerospace industry continues to evolve, Safran and CFM International are well-positioned to capitalize on new opportunities and continue to drive innovation in the sector.
