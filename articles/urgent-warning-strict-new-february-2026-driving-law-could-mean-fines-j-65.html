What are the Key Changes in the February 2026 Driving Law?

The new law introduces several significant changes to existing driving regulations. One of the most notable changes is the increase in fines for speeding. Drivers caught speeding will face fines of up to $1,000, which is a significant increase from the previous limit of $500. Additionally, the law introduces a new point system, where drivers accumulate points for each violation. If a driver accumulates 12 points or more within a year, their license will be suspended for 6 months.

Another critical aspect of the new law is the mandatory use of seatbelts. Drivers and passengers must wear seatbelts at all times while the vehicle is in motion. Failure to comply with this regulation could result in a fine of up to $200. The law also introduces stricter regulations for driving under the influence. Drivers caught driving under the influence will face jail time of up to 2 years and a fine of up to $5,000.

The new law also focuses on promoting road safety through technology. Vehicles manufactured after 2025 must be equipped with advanced safety features such as lane departure warning systems and blind spot detection. This move is expected to reduce the number of accidents caused by human error.

How Will the New Law be Enforced?

The enforcement of the new law will be carried out by a combination of traffic cameras and police patrols. The government has invested in advanced traffic management systems that use AI-powered cameras to detect and record driving violations. The cameras will be able to detect speeding, lane departure, and other violations, and will automatically issue fines to violators.

In addition to traffic cameras, police patrols will also play a crucial role in enforcing the new law. Police officers will be equipped with handheld devices that can detect and record driving violations. The devices will be able to scan license plates and driver's licenses, making it easier for officers to issue fines and penalties.

The government has also established a dedicated task force to handle the enforcement of the new law. The task force will be responsible for monitoring traffic cameras and police patrols, as well as investigating accidents and issuing fines.
