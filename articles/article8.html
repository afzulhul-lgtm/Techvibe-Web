Over 65 and Feeling Stiff? New Research Shows It May Not Be Your Muscles at Fault

By Sarah Mitchell
23 Feb, 2026

As we age, it's common to feel a little stiffer and less flexible. We often blame our muscles, thinking that years of wear and tear have taken their toll. But what if it's not your muscles that are the problem? New research is shedding light on the importance of connective tissue in maintaining joint flexibility as we age. This breakthrough has significant implications for senior health and how we approach aging well in 2026.

Understanding Connective Tissue

Connective tissue is the glue that holds our bodies together, providing support and structure to our joints, bones, and muscles. As we age, this tissue can become less flexible and more prone to injury, leading to stiffness and pain. But by understanding how to maintain healthy connective tissue, we can take steps to stay flexible and mobile well into old age.
