Maruti Suzuki Jimny new 5 door mini SUV comes with luxury features, check the price

By Mike Reynolds Senior Editor
Published: May 06, 2026 Updated: May 06, 2026 7 Min Read

The Indian automotive market is abuzz with the launch of the Maruti Suzuki Jimny, a 5-door mini SUV that promises to bring luxury features to the masses. As the latest entrant in the compact SUV segment, the Jimny is poised to take on established players like the Mahindra Thar and the Ford EcoSport. With its rugged design and impressive off-road capabilities, the Jimny is likely to appeal to adventure-seekers and urban dwellers alike.

The Jimny's design is a notable aspect of its appeal, with a boxy silhouette and a flat hood that gives it a distinctly utilitarian look. The interior, on the other hand, is a study in contrasts, with premium materials and modern features like a touchscreen infotainment system and cruise control. The Jimny's dimensions are compact, making it an ideal choice for city driving, but its high ground clearance and four-wheel drive capability make it more than capable of handling rough terrain.

Estimates suggest that the Maruti Suzuki Jimny will be priced competitively, with prices starting from approximately ₹10 lakh for the base model. The top-of-the-line variant, which comes with features like a leather-wrapped steering wheel and a rearview camera, is likely to cost around ₹15 lakh. In comparison, the Mahindra Thar is priced between ₹10 lakh and ₹14 lakh, while the Ford EcoSport ranges from ₹8 lakh to ₹12 lakh.

The Jimny's engine options are likely to include a 1.5-liter petrol unit and a 1.3-liter diesel unit, both of which will be paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. The petrol engine is expected to produce around 100 horsepower, while the diesel engine will reportedly produce around 90 horsepower. In terms of fuel efficiency, the Jimny is likely to deliver around 15-18 kilometers per liter in the city and around 20-22 kilometers per liter on the highway.

The Jimny's off-road capabilities are a major selling point, with features like a high ground clearance, a rear differential lock, and a four-wheel drive system. The SUV also comes with a range of safety features, including dual airbags, anti-lock braking, and electronic stability control. In terms of competition, the Jimny will be up against the likes of the Mahindra Thar, the Ford EcoSport, and the Hyundai Venue, all of which offer a similar blend of style, substance, and capability.
