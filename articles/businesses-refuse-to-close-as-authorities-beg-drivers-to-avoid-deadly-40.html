Businesses Refuse to Close as Authorities Beg Drivers to Avoid Deadly Snow-Covered Roads

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: April 27, 2026 Updated: April 27, 2026 6 Min Read

As a severe snowstorm sweeps across the region, authorities are urging drivers to exercise extreme caution and avoid traveling unless absolutely necessary. The heavy snowfall has resulted in treacherous road conditions, with icy roads, reduced visibility, and a high risk of accidents. Despite the warnings, some businesses are refusing to close, citing concerns about lost productivity and revenue.

The decision by some businesses to remain open has sparked a heated debate about the balance between economic interests and public safety. While some argue that businesses have a responsibility to prioritize the safety of their employees and customers, others believe that they should be allowed to make their own decisions about whether to stay open or close. As the situation continues to unfold, it remains to be seen how the authorities will respond to the refusal of some businesses to close, and what measures they will take to ensure public safety.

Understanding the Risks of Snow-Covered Roads

The risks associated with driving on snow-covered roads are well-documented. Reduced traction, decreased visibility, and increased stopping distances all contribute to a heightened risk of accidents. According to data from the Federal Highway Administration, over 1,300 people are killed each year in the United States as a result of vehicle crashes on snowy or icy roads. Furthermore, nearly 116,000 people are injured in such crashes, resulting in significant economic and social costs.

Despite these risks, some drivers may feel pressured to venture out onto the roads, particularly if they are required to commute to work or attend to other essential duties. However, authorities are urging drivers to exercise extreme caution and to consider alternative arrangements, such as working from home or postponing non-essential travel, until the weather conditions improve.

In addition to the risks associated with driving on snow-covered roads, there are also concerns about the impact of severe weather conditions on vulnerable populations, such as the elderly and those with disabilities. These individuals may be more susceptible to the effects of cold weather and may require additional support and assistance during periods of severe weather.

The Role of Authorities in Ensuring Public Safety

Authorities play a critical role in ensuring public safety during periods of severe weather. This includes providing timely and accurate information about weather conditions, road closures, and other hazards, as well as implementing measures to reduce the risks associated with driving on snow-covered roads. Such measures may include reducing speed limits, increasing the frequency of road maintenance, and deploying emergency services personnel to strategic locations.

In addition to these measures, authorities may also need to take a more proactive approach to ensuring that businesses prioritize the safety of their employees and customers. This could involve working with businesses to develop emergency plans and procedures, providing guidance on how to respond to severe weather conditions, and enforcing regulations and laws related to workplace safety.

Some examples of measures that authorities can take to ensure public safety include:

  • Implementing emergency alert systems to quickly disseminate information about severe weather conditions and other hazards
  • Deploying road crews to clear snow and ice from roads and highways
  • Providing support and assistance to vulnerable populations, such as the elderly and those with disabilities

The Impact of Severe Weather on Businesses and the Economy

Severe weather conditions can have a significant impact on businesses and the economy, particularly if they are forced to close or reduce operations. According to a study by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), the economic impacts of severe weather events can be substantial, with some events resulting in losses of over $1 billion. Furthermore, the study found that the economic impacts of severe weather events can be felt for an extended period, with some businesses taking months or even years to recover.

In addition to the economic impacts, severe weather conditions can also have a significant impact on the supply chain, with disruptions to transportation and logistics resulting in delays and increased costs. This can have a ripple effect throughout the economy, with businesses and consumers alike feeling the impacts of severe weather conditions.
