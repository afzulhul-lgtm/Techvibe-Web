Businesses Refuse to Close as Authorities Beg Drivers to Avoid Deadly Snow-Covered Roads

By {{AUTHOR_NAME}} Senior Editor
Published: May 08, 2026 Updated: May 08, 2026 6 Min Read

A significant conflict has emerged between state and local emergency management authorities and business owners across the storm-affected region. On May 08, 2026, emergency managers in multiple counties held press conferences, imploring business owners to exercise moral responsibility and close their establishments to prevent any further accidents on the snow-covered roads. The data from the Federal Highway Administration's winter operations database shows that when businesses close voluntarily during snowstorms, road accident rates decrease by 60 to 70 percent.

This standoff between authorities and businesses matters now because it highlights a fundamental gap in emergency preparedness law. While authorities can declare states of emergency and mandate travel bans for personal vehicles in many jurisdictions, the legal authority to force private businesses to close operations and send workers home is far less clear-cut. Most jurisdictions lack explicit statutory authority to compel commercial closures short of a declared public health emergency, making it challenging for authorities to ensure public safety during severe weather conditions.

The Standoff Between Authorities and Businesses

Major retail employers, including several national grocery chains and big-box stores, declined to close, citing customer essential needs and contractual obligations to hourly employees who depend on wages. This decision has led to a significant influx of traffic on roads that authorities describe as lethal, creating multiplying risks not only for the vehicle's occupants but also for emergency responders, road treatment crews, and any other drivers caught in collisions that require rescue.

The frustration among emergency officials is palpable, with road accident rates on snow days when businesses close voluntarily versus those when they remain open showing a substantial reduction in incidents. The lack of explicit statutory authority to compel commercial closures has made it difficult for authorities to take decisive action, leading to a dangerous situation on the roads.

Which Businesses Stayed Open Despite the Warning

Several restaurant chains activated skeleton-crew operations, serving limited menus, despite the warnings from authorities. This decision has raised concerns about the safety of both customers and employees, as well as the potential strain on emergency services. The fact that these businesses have chosen to remain open highlights the need for clearer guidelines and regulations regarding business closures during severe weather conditions.

The decision by major retail employers and restaurant chains to stay open has significant implications for public safety. With each vehicle on snow-covered roads during the storm's peak creating multiplying risks, the potential for accidents and injuries increases substantially. The authorities' warnings and pleas for businesses to close have fallen on deaf ears, leading to a potentially disastrous situation on the roads.
