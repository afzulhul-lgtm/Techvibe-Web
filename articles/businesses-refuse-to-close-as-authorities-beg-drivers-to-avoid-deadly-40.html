What are the Risks of Traveling on Snow-Covered Roads?

Traveling on snow-covered roads can be extremely hazardous, with 1 in 5 accidents resulting in serious injuries or fatalities. The main risks include reduced visibility, slippery surfaces, and increased stopping distances. According to a study by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA), 17,000 people were killed in vehicle crashes on snowy or icy roads between 2015 and 2019.

To mitigate these risks, it's essential to take necessary precautions, such as slowing down, using low gear, and increasing following distances. Additionally, 70% of vehicles are not equipped with winter tires, which can significantly improve traction and control on snowy roads. By taking these precautions and being aware of the risks, drivers can reduce their chances of being involved in an accident.

A 4,500mAh battery-powered winter kit, including a flashlight, first aid kit, and warm blanket, can also be a lifesaver in case of an emergency. Furthermore, 80% of businesses are now using telematics and fleet management systems to monitor their vehicles and drivers in real-time, reducing the risk of accidents and improving response times.
