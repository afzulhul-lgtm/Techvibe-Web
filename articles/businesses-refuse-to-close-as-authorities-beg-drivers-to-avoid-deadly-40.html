Businesses Refuse to Close as Authorities Beg Drivers to Avoid Deadly Snow-Covered Roads

By Rachel Wong Senior Editor
Published: May 06, 2026 Updated: May 06, 2026 8 Min Read

On days like May 6, 2026, when a severe snow storm hits, the usual bustle of commercial activity can become a matter of life and death. As authorities beg drivers to avoid deadly snow-covered roads, some businesses are refusing to close, citing the need to maintain operations and serve their customers. This stance has led to a conflict between business owners and local authorities, who are concerned about the safety of employees and customers alike.

The situation highlights the delicate balance between economic necessity and public safety. While businesses may feel pressure to stay open to minimize losses and maintain customer loyalty, authorities must prioritize the safety of the community. As the snow storm continues to wreak havoc on the roads, the question on everyone's mind is: what is the best course of action for businesses, employees, and drivers?

What the Authorities Are Saying

Local authorities have issued stern warnings, advising drivers to avoid all non-essential travel and to exercise extreme caution when navigating snow-covered roads. They have also urged businesses to consider the safety of their employees and customers, and to take necessary precautions to prevent accidents. The authorities have reportedly set up emergency services and snow removal teams to help mitigate the effects of the storm.

However, despite these efforts, the roads remain treacherous, with estimates suggesting that hundreds of accidents have occurred since the storm began. The authorities are working around the clock to clear the roads and restore normal traffic flow, but the situation remains precarious. As the storm shows no signs of abating, the authorities are becoming increasingly concerned about the safety of the community.

The authorities have also announced plans to impose penalties on businesses that fail to comply with safety regulations, including fines and potential closures. This move is intended to encourage businesses to prioritize the safety of their employees and customers, and to take necessary precautions to prevent accidents.

Why Businesses Are Refusing to Close

Businesses are refusing to close for a variety of reasons, including the need to maintain operations, serve customers, and minimize losses. Some businesses, such as hospitals and emergency services, are essential and cannot afford to close, even in the face of severe weather conditions. Others, such as retail stores and restaurants, may feel pressure to stay open to maintain customer loyalty and prevent losses.

Additionally, some businesses may not have the necessary infrastructure or resources to support remote work, making it difficult for them to close without significantly disrupting their operations. In these cases, businesses may feel that they have no choice but to stay open, despite the risks. However, this decision can have serious consequences, including putting the lives of employees and customers at risk.

It is worth noting that some businesses are taking steps to mitigate the risks associated with staying open during a snow storm. For example, some companies are offering flexible work arrangements, such as telecommuting or flexible hours, to help employees avoid traveling during hazardous conditions. Others are providing additional safety measures, such as snow removal services or emergency kits, to help employees and customers stay safe.

How Dangerous Are the Roads

The roads are extremely dangerous, with estimates suggesting that the risk of accidents is significantly higher during snow storms. The combination of icy roads, reduced visibility, and hazardous driving conditions makes it difficult for drivers to navigate, even at slow speeds. Additionally, the risk of black ice, avalanches, and other hazards makes the roads even more treacherous.

According to reports, the roads are experiencing significant disruptions, with many roads closed or impassable due to heavy snowfall and ice. The authorities are working to clear the roads, but the process is slow and laborious, and it may take several days or even weeks to restore normal traffic flow. In the meantime, drivers are advised to exercise extreme caution and to avoid all non-essential travel.

The situation is further complicated by the fact that many drivers are not properly equipped to handle hazardous driving conditions. For example, some drivers may not have snow tires or chains, which can significantly reduce traction and increase the risk of accidents. Additionally, some drivers may not be familiar with the roads or the weather conditions, which can make it difficult for them to navigate safely.

The Legal Question — Are Businesses Required to Close

The legal question of whether businesses are required to close during a snow storm is complex and depends on various factors, including the nature of the business, the severity of the weather conditions, and the specific laws and regulations in place. In general, businesses are not required to close during a snow storm, but they may be required to take certain precautions to ensure the safety of their employees and customers.

For example, businesses may be required to provide a safe working environment, which includes ensuring that the premises are free from hazards and that employees are properly trained and equipped to handle emergency situations. Additionally, businesses may be required to comply with specific regulations, such as those related to snow removal or emergency preparedness.

However, the laws and regulations surrounding business closures during snow storms vary widely depending on the jurisdiction. In some cases, businesses may be required to close if the authorities deem it necessary to protect public safety. In other cases, businesses may be allowed to stay open, but may be required to take certain precautions to ensure the safety of their employees and customers.

What Drivers and Employees Should Do

Drivers and employees should take necessary precautions to ensure their safety during a snow storm. This includes checking the weather forecast and road conditions before traveling, using caution when driving, and avoiding all non-essential travel. Additionally, drivers should ensure that their vehicles are properly equipped for hazardous driving conditions, including having snow tires, chains, and emergency kits.

Employees should also take necessary precautions to ensure their safety, including following company policies and procedures for emergency situations, staying informed about weather conditions and road closures, and taking regular breaks to rest and stay warm. Additionally, employees should be aware of their rights and responsibilities during a snow storm, including the right to refuse to work in hazardous conditions.

It is also important for drivers and employees to stay informed about the latest developments and updates from the authorities. This includes checking social media, news websites, and emergency alert systems for updates on road closures, weather conditions, and other important information.
