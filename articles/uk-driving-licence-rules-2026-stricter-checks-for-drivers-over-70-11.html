In response to growing concerns about road safety, the UK government has introduced new rules for drivers over 70. The UK driving licence rules 2026 aim to ensure that older drivers are fit to drive, reducing the risk of accidents on the road. With the DVLA over 70 renewal process becoming more rigorous, it's essential for senior drivers to understand the new regulations.

What's Changing for Drivers Over 70?

The new rules require drivers over 70 to undergo stricter medical checks to renew their licence. This includes assessments for vision, cognitive function, and physical ability. The goal is to identify potential health issues that could impact driving skills, such as dementia or eyesight problems. Drivers who fail to meet the medical standards may face a driving ban over 70 UK, so it's crucial to stay informed about the new DVLA rules 2026.
