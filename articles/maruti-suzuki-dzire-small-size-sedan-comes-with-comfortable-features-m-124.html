Maruti Suzuki Dzire small size sedan comes with comfortable features , mileage is high

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: April 08, 2026 Updated: April 08, 2026 6 Min Read

What makes the Maruti Suzuki Dzire a top-selling small size sedan in India? Is it the high mileage of up to 24.12 kmpl, or the comfortable features that come standard with the vehicle? As a seasoned automotive expert, I've taken a closer look at the Maruti Suzuki Dzire to find out what sets it apart from the competition.

The Maruti Suzuki Dzire has been a staple in the Indian automotive market for years, and its popularity can be attributed to its affordable price of ₹ 6.09 lakh and ₹ 9.13 lakh for the base and top models, respectively. But what really makes the Dzire stand out is its unique blend of comfort, style, and performance. With a 1.2-liter petrol engine and a 5-speed manual transmission, the Dzire is perfect for city driving and long road trips alike.

What are the Key Features of the Maruti Suzuki Dzire?

The Maruti Suzuki Dzire comes with a range of comfortable features, including power windows, central locking, and keyless entry. The vehicle also features a 7-inch touchscreen infotainment system with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto connectivity, making it easy to stay connected on the go. In terms of safety, the Dzire has a 5-star rating from the Global New Car Assessment Programme (GNCAP) and comes standard with ABS and EBD.

One of the standout features of the Maruti Suzuki Dzire is its high mileage. With a claimed mileage of up to 24.12 kmpl, the Dzire is perfect for those who want to save on fuel costs. The vehicle also has a 37-liter fuel tank, making it ideal for long road trips. According to Rajesh Lal, Senior Manager at Maruti Suzuki, "The Dzire is designed to provide a perfect blend of comfort, style, and performance, making it a top choice for Indian car buyers."

In terms of variants, the Maruti Suzuki Dzire is available in 4 trim levels: LXi, VXi, ZXi, and ZXi+. Each variant comes with its own unique set of features, including alloy wheels, rear parking sensors, and a rearview camera. The top-of-the-line ZXi+ variant also comes with LED headlights and a leather-wrapped steering wheel.

The Maruti Suzuki Dzire has a number of competitors in the Indian market, including the Hyundai Xcent and the Tata Tigor.
