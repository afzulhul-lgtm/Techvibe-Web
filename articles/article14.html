Sweet Potatoes vs. Regular Potatoes: Why Science Says They Aren't Closely Related at All

By Sarah Mitchell
23 Feb, 2026

When it comes to potatoes, most of us assume that sweet potatoes and regular potatoes are closely related - after all, they're both spuds, right? But the truth is, these two types of potatoes are actually quite distant cousins. In fact, from a plant science perspective, they're not even in the same family. While regular potatoes belong to the Solanum tuberosum family, sweet potatoes are part of the Ipomoea batatas family. This difference in classification has significant implications for their nutritional content, taste, and even how they're grown.

Understanding the Basics of Potato Varieties

So, what exactly are the key differences between these two types of potatoes? For starters, sweet potatoes are much higher in fiber and vitamins A and C, making them a popular choice for health-conscious eaters. Regular potatoes, on the other hand, are higher in starch and calories. When it comes to food facts, it's clear that sweet potatoes are the clear winner in terms of nutritional value.

Exploring the Science Behind Potato Classification

But why are sweet potatoes and regular potatoes so different, despite both being types of potatoes? The answer lies in agricultural biology. Sweet potatoes are native to the Americas, while regular potatoes originated in South America. Over time, these two types of potatoes evolved separately, developing distinct characteristics and nutritional profiles. This separation is reflected in their classification as distinct species.

Whether you're a fan of sweet potatoes or regular potatoes, it's clear that both have their own special place in the world of food and agricultural biology.
