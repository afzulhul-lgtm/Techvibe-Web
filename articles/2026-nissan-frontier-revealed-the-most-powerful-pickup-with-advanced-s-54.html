Power and Performance

The 2026 Nissan Frontier boasts an impressive 3.8-liter V6 engine, capable of producing 310 horsepower and 281 lb-ft of torque. But that's not the whole picture. The truck's advanced transmission system and cleverly designed drivetrain work together to deliver smooth, responsive acceleration and exceptional towing capacity. What changed? The new Frontier's engine and transmission have been optimized for better fuel efficiency and reduced emissions.

Here's the thing: the Frontier's power and performance are not just about raw numbers. The truck's advanced engineering and design features, such as its hydraulic body mount system and advanced suspension, work together to provide a comfortable and composed ride, even in the most challenging driving conditions. The numbers tell a different story, too: the Frontier's maximum towing capacity is an impressive 6,640 pounds, making it an ideal choice for those who need a reliable workhorse.

Safety Features

The 2026 Nissan Frontier is equipped with an impressive array of advanced safety features, including automatic emergency braking, lane departure warning, and blind spot monitoring. But what sets the Frontier apart is its innovative use of camera and sensor technology to provide a 360-degree view of the surrounding environment. That's where it gets complicated: the truck's advanced safety features are not just about preventing accidents, but also about providing drivers with greater confidence and control on the road.

The Frontier's safety features are designed to work together seamlessly, providing a comprehensive safety net that protects drivers and passengers alike. From its advanced airbag system to its cleverly designed crumple zones, the Frontier is built to withstand even the most severe impacts. The result? A truck that is not only powerful and capable but also remarkably safe.
