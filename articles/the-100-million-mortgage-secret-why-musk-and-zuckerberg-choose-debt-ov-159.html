The $100 Million Mortgage Secret: Why Musk and Zuckerberg Choose Debt Over Cash

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: April 28, 2026 Updated: April 28, 2026 7 Min Read

When it comes to managing their wealth, high-profile individuals like Elon Musk and Mark Zuckerberg have a surprising strategy in common: they choose debt over cash. This approach may seem counterintuitive, especially considering their immense wealth, but it is a deliberate choice that reflects a sophisticated understanding of financial management. By opting for significant mortgages, these individuals are able to leverage their debt to achieve specific financial goals.

At first glance, it may seem puzzling that billionaires like Musk and Zuckerberg would choose to take on debt when they have ample cash reserves. However, their decision is rooted in a nuanced understanding of the benefits of borrowing. By using debt to finance their investments and lifestyle, they are able to free up their cash for more strategic uses, such as investing in their businesses or pursuing new ventures. This approach allows them to maximize their returns and build their wealth over time.

The Benefits of Debt for High-Net-Worth Individuals

There are several key benefits that make debt an attractive option for high-net-worth individuals like Musk and Zuckerberg. One of the primary advantages is the ability to leverage their debt to achieve a higher return on investment. By borrowing at a relatively low interest rate, they can invest their cash in assets that generate a higher return, such as stocks or real estate. This strategy allows them to build their wealth more efficiently and effectively.

Another benefit of debt for high-net-worth individuals is the tax advantages it offers. In many cases, the interest paid on a mortgage or other loan is tax-deductible, which can help reduce their tax liability. This can be especially beneficial for individuals who are subject to high tax rates, as it allows them to minimize their tax burden and retain more of their wealth. By using debt strategically, Musk and Zuckerberg are able to optimize their tax situation and build their wealth over time.

It's also worth noting that high-net-worth individuals often have access to more favorable loan terms, which can make debt an even more attractive option. They may be able to negotiate lower interest rates or more flexible repayment terms, which can help reduce their borrowing costs and increase their returns. This can be especially beneficial for individuals who are pursuing large-scale investments or business ventures, as it allows them to manage their cash flow more effectively.

How Debt Fits into a Broader Financial Strategy

For high-net-worth individuals like Musk and Zuckerberg, debt is just one part of a broader financial strategy. They typically have a diversified portfolio of assets, including stocks, bonds, and real estate, which are designed to generate returns and build their wealth over time. By using debt strategically, they are able to optimize their portfolio and achieve their financial goals more efficiently.

A key part of this strategy is asset allocation, which involves dividing their portfolio among different asset classes to manage risk and maximize returns. By allocating their assets effectively, high-net-worth individuals can reduce their exposure to market volatility and increase their potential for long-term growth. Debt can play a critical role in this process, as it allows them to leverage their assets and achieve a higher return on investment.

In addition to asset allocation, high-net-worth individuals also need to consider tax planning and risk management when developing their financial strategy. By using debt strategically, they can minimize their tax liability and reduce their exposure to potential risks, such as market downturns or business failures. This allows them to build their wealth more sustainably and achieve their long-term financial goals.

The Role of Mortgages in High-Net-Worth Financial Planning

Mortgages can play a significant role in the financial planning of high-net-worth individuals, as they offer a way to leverage debt and achieve specific financial goals. By using a mortgage to finance the purchase of a home or investment property, individuals like Musk and Zuckerberg can free up their cash for more strategic uses, such as investing in their businesses or pursuing new ventures.

One of the key benefits of mortgages for high-net-worth individuals is the ability to leverage their equity and achieve a higher return on investment. By borrowing against the value of their property, they can access cash for other investments or business opportunities, which can help build their wealth more efficiently. This approach can be especially beneficial for individuals who are pursuing large-scale investments or business ventures, as it allows them to manage their cash flow more effectively.
