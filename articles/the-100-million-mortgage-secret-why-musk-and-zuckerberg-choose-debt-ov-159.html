The $100 Million Mortgage Secret: Why Musk and Zuckerberg Choose Debt Over Cash

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: May 06, 2026 Updated: May 06, 2026 6 Min Read

High-net-worth individuals, such as billionaires Elon Musk and Mark Zuckerberg, often opt for taking out mortgages instead of paying cash for their properties. This phenomenon raises questions about the reasoning behind such a decision, especially when these individuals have the financial means to afford outright purchases.

The strategy of choosing debt over cash for real estate purchases is not new, but it has garnered significant attention in recent years due to its adoption by prominent figures. Understanding the motivations and potential benefits of this approach can provide insights into the financial planning and wealth management strategies of the ultra-wealthy.

What the Mortgage Secret Actually Is

The "mortgage secret" refers to the practice of wealthy individuals taking out mortgages on their properties, even when they have sufficient funds to pay cash. This strategy is often rooted in tax benefits and wealth management principles. By borrowing money to purchase a property, these individuals can potentially reduce their tax liabilities and free up their cash for other investments or financial pursuits.

Estimates suggest that the wealthy often prioritize investment returns over outright property ownership. By using debt to finance their real estate purchases, they can allocate their cash to higher-yielding investments, such as stocks, bonds, or business ventures. This approach allows them to potentially grow their wealth more efficiently than if they were to tie up their capital in property purchases.

Why Billionaires Borrow Instead of Paying Cash

Billionaires like Musk and Zuckerberg may choose to borrow instead of paying cash due to the potential tax advantages associated with mortgage interest deductions. In the United States, for example, homeowners can deduct the interest paid on their mortgages from their taxable income, which can result in significant tax savings. By taking out a mortgage, these individuals can reduce their tax liabilities and retain more of their wealth.

Another reason billionaires might prefer borrowing over paying cash is to maintain liquidity. By not tying up their capital in a single asset, such as a property, they can respond more easily to changing market conditions or new investment opportunities. This flexibility is crucial for high-net-worth individuals who often have complex financial portfolios and multiple business interests.

How This Tax and Wealth Strategy Works

The strategy of using debt to finance property purchases involves careful planning and consideration of various factors, including interest rates, tax laws, and investment opportunities. Wealthy individuals typically work with financial advisors and tax professionals to optimize their mortgage and investment strategies, ensuring that they are maximizing their returns while minimizing their tax liabilities.

Typically, the process begins with a thorough analysis of the individual's financial situation, including their income, expenses, assets, and liabilities. Based on this analysis, their advisors can recommend the most suitable mortgage products and investment vehicles, taking into account factors such as interest rates, loan terms, and potential returns.

Can Regular People Apply This Strategy

While the strategy of choosing debt over cash for property purchases is often associated with the ultra-wealthy, regular people can also apply similar principles to their own financial planning. However, it is essential to approach this strategy with caution and consider individual circumstances, such as income stability, creditworthiness, and financial goals.

For example, individuals with stable incomes and good credit scores may be able to secure favorable mortgage terms, allowing them to borrow at relatively low interest rates. By using debt to finance their property purchases, they can potentially free up their cash for other investments or expenses, such as retirement savings or education costs.

What Financial Experts Say About It

Financial experts generally agree that the strategy of choosing debt over cash for property purchases can be effective for wealthy individuals, but it is not without risks. They emphasize the importance of careful planning, consideration of individual circumstances, and ongoing monitoring of market conditions and tax laws.

Estimates suggest that the majority of high-net-worth individuals use some form of debt financing for their property purchases, highlighting the popularity of this strategy among the ultra-wealthy. However, financial experts also caution that this approach may not be suitable for everyone, particularly those with unstable incomes or high levels of debt.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the tax benefits of taking out a mortgage?

The tax benefits of taking out a mortgage include the ability to deduct mortgage interest from taxable income, which can result in significant tax savings. Additionally, property taxes can also be deducted, further reducing tax liabilities.

How do interest rates affect the strategy of choosing debt over cash for mortgages?

Interest rates play a significant role in the strategy of choosing debt over cash for mortgages. When interest rates are low, borrowing becomes more attractive, as the cost of debt is reduced. Conversely, when interest rates are high, the cost of debt increases, making it less desirable to borrow.

Conclusion

The strategy of choosing debt over cash for mortgages is a complex and multifaceted approach that involves careful planning and consideration of various factors. Ultimately, the decision to use debt to finance property purchases depends on a range of factors, including income stability, creditworthiness, and financial goals.
