2026 Toyota Prado Revealed: The Next-Gen Ultimate SUV with Bold Design & Unmatched Power

By Marcus Webb Senior Editor
Published: May 10, 2026 Updated: May 10, 2026 5 Min Read

The 2026 Toyota Prado revealed is making waves in the automotive world, and for good reason. This next-gen SUV boasts a bold design and unmatched power, courtesy of its 2.4-liter turbocharged four-cylinder engine, which produces 278 horsepower. But why does this matter? The answer lies in its ability to deliver a seamless driving experience, both on and off the road.

So, what's new with the 2026 Toyota Prado? For starters, it's built on the TNGA-F platform, which was introduced in the 2024 model year. This fifth-generation Prado also features updated powertrain options, including a hybrid system in select markets, which bumps up the horsepower to 309. And, in some markets, including Australia, the Middle East, and Europe, a twin-turbodiesel 2.8-liter four-cylinder producing 204 horsepower is also available.

Key Specs and Features Breakdown

Here's the thing: the 2026 Toyota Prado revealed is not just about power; it's also about features. Four-wheel drive is standard, along with a two-speed transfer case. The Kinetic Dynamic Suspension System with active roll control is also standard on higher trims, which improves both on-road handling and off-road articulation. And, with Crawl Control and Multi-Terrain Select, you'll be well-equipped to tackle even the toughest terrain.

That's where it gets interesting. The 2026 update adds a 12.3-inch touchscreen with Toyota's latest multimedia system, as well as enhanced Toyota Safety Sense 3.0 across the range. Interior seating for 7 in the three-row configuration is available, making it an ideal choice for families or groups. From what's been reported so far, the 2026 Toyota Prado revealed is shaping up to be a top contender in the premium off-road SUV segment.

How It Compares to the Previous Model

To put that in perspective, the 2026 Toyota Prado revealed is a significant improvement over its predecessor. With its updated powertrain options and advanced safety features, it's clear that Toyota is committed to staying ahead of the curve. But, is it enough to take on the likes of the Ford Bronco, Land Rover Defender, and Jeep Wrangler? That's not entirely clear yet, but one thing is certain: the 2026 Toyota Prado revealed is a force to be reckoned with.

Worth noting is the fact that the 2026 Toyota Prado revealed is priced competitively, with Australian pricing starting at AUD 67,000 for the GX diesel and climbing to AUD 93,000 for the VX hybrid. In the Middle East, the base GX retails from approximately $55,000 equivalent. It's hard to say exactly why the pricing varies so much, but it's likely due to a combination of factors, including local market conditions and regulatory requirements.
