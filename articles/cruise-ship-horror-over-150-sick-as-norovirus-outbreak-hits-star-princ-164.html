Cruise Ship Horror: Over 150 Sick as Norovirus Outbreak Hits Star Princess During 7-Day Voyage

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: May 06, 2026 Updated: May 06, 2026 8 Min Read

The norovirus, a highly contagious virus, is known for causing severe gastrointestinal symptoms, including diarrhea, vomiting, and stomach cramps. On cruise ships, where people are in close proximity to one another, the risk of outbreak is heightened. The Star Princess, a cruise ship that embarked on a 7-day voyage, has reportedly been affected by such an outbreak, with over 150 passengers falling ill.

The norovirus is typically spread through contaminated food, water, or surfaces, as well as through direct contact with an infected person. On cruise ships, the virus can spread quickly due to the close living quarters and shared facilities. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) have guidelines in place for cruise ships to follow in the event of a norovirus outbreak, including increased sanitation and hygiene practices.

What Happened Aboard the Star Princess

The Star Princess, a cruise ship owned and operated by Princess Cruises, set sail on its 7-day voyage with thousands of passengers on board. Shortly after departure, passengers began to fall ill, exhibiting symptoms of norovirus infection. The ship's crew and medical staff responded quickly, implementing measures to contain the outbreak and prevent further spread of the virus.

As the outbreak worsened, the ship's crew increased sanitation and hygiene practices, including thorough cleaning and disinfection of all surfaces and facilities. Passengers were also advised to take precautions, such as frequent handwashing and avoiding close contact with anyone who was sick. Despite these efforts, the number of cases continued to rise, with over 150 passengers eventually falling ill.

The norovirus outbreak on the Star Princess is not an isolated incident. According to the CDC, norovirus is the leading cause of gastroenteritis outbreaks on cruise ships, accounting for approximately 90% of all reported outbreaks. The CDC works closely with cruise lines to investigate and respond to outbreaks, and provides guidance on prevention and control measures.

How Norovirus Spread Through the Ship

Norovirus can spread quickly on cruise ships due to the close living quarters and shared facilities. The virus can be transmitted through contaminated food, water, or surfaces, as well as through direct contact with an infected person. On the Star Princess, the virus likely spread through a combination of these routes, with passengers and crew members unknowingly carrying and transmitting the virus.

The ship's galley, or kitchen, is a potential hotspot for norovirus transmission, as food handlers may contaminate food and surfaces. Additionally, the ship's bathrooms and public areas, such as restaurants and lounges, can also facilitate the spread of the virus. The CDC recommends that cruise ships take measures to prevent the spread of norovirus, including implementing proper food handling and preparation practices, increasing sanitation and hygiene practices, and providing education to passengers and crew on norovirus prevention.

Cruise ships can take steps to reduce the risk of norovirus transmission, such as increasing the frequency of cleaning and disinfection, providing hand sanitizer stations throughout the ship, and implementing sick leave policies for crew members. Passengers can also take precautions, such as frequent handwashing, avoiding close contact with anyone who is sick, and reporting any symptoms to the ship's medical staff.

To protect yourself from norovirus on a cruise, it is essential to take precautions such as frequent handwashing, avoiding close contact with anyone who is sick, and avoiding contaminated food and water. Passengers can also take steps to reduce their risk of infection, such as avoiding raw or undercooked shellfish, and avoiding close contact with anyone who has recently been sick.

Cruise ships can also provide education and support to passengers on norovirus prevention, including providing hand sanitizer stations and increasing sanitation and hygiene practices. The CDC recommends that passengers take precautions to protect themselves from norovirus, and follow the advice of the ship's medical staff if they fall ill.

Some additional measures that passengers can take to protect themselves from norovirus on cruises include:

  • Washing hands frequently with soap and water, especially after using the bathroom and before eating.
  • Avoiding close contact with anyone who is sick.
  • Avoiding contaminated food and water.
  • Following proper food handling and preparation practices.
  • Staying hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids.
