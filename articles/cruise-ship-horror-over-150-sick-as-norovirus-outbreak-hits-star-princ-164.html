Cruise Ship Horror: Over 150 Sick as Norovirus Outbreak Hits Star Princess During 7-Day Voyage

By Sarah Mitchell Senior Tech Editor
Published: March 15, 2026 Updated: March 15, 2026 5 Min Read

A highly contagious norovirus outbreak has struck the Star Princess cruise ship, leaving over 150 passengers and crew members sick during a 7-day voyage in the Caribbean. The outbreak, which was reported to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), has raised concerns about the spread of illnesses on cruise ships and the effectiveness of sanitation measures. As the ship docked at Port Everglades in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, passengers and crew members were met with a team of CDC investigators who were on board to assess the situation and provide guidance on how to contain the outbreak.

Introduction to Norovirus Outbreaks on Cruise Ships

Norovirus outbreaks on cruise ships are not uncommon, and the CDC has reported several incidents in recent years. The virus is highly contagious and can spread quickly through contaminated food, water, and surfaces. According to the CDC, norovirus is the leading cause of gastroenteritis outbreaks on cruise ships, and it is estimated that over 20 million people in the United States contract norovirus each year. The symptoms of norovirus include vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps, and fever, and can last for several days.

The Star Princess outbreak occurred during a 7-day voyage in the Caribbean, and it is believed that the virus was introduced to the ship through an infected passenger or crew member. The ship's crew took measures to contain the outbreak, including increasing sanitation and hygiene protocols, and providing medical care to those who were affected. However, despite these efforts, the outbreak continued to spread, and the CDC was notified. The CDC sent a team of investigators to the ship to assess the situation and provide guidance on how to contain the outbreak.

"The health and safety of our passengers and crew is our top priority, and we are working closely with the CDC to contain the outbreak and prevent further spread of the virus," said a spokesperson for Princess Cruises.

Symptoms and Treatment of Norovirus

The symptoms of norovirus can be severe and debilitating, and can last for several days. Treatment for norovirus typically involves rest, hydration, and over-the-counter medications to manage symptoms. In severe cases, hospitalization may be necessary. The CDC recommends that people who are infected with norovirus stay hydrated by drinking plenty of fluids, and avoid close contact with others to prevent the spread of the virus.

CDC Investigation and Response

The CDC investigation into the Star Princess outbreak involved a team of epidemiologists and environmental health specialists who assessed the ship's sanitation and hygiene protocols, and interviewed passengers and crew members who were affected by the outbreak. The CDC also collected specimens from affected individuals to test for norovirus. The results of the investigation will be used to determine the cause of the outbreak and to provide recommendations for preventing future outbreaks.

Environmental Assessment

The CDC conducted an environmental assessment of the ship to identify potential sources of contamination. This included testing of food, water, and surfaces for norovirus, as well as an evaluation of the ship's sanitation and hygiene protocols. The CDC also reviewed the ship's cleaning and disinfection procedures to ensure that they were adequate to prevent the spread of the virus.

Prevention and Mitigation of Norovirus Outbreaks on Cruise Ships

Preventing and mitigating norovirus outbreaks on cruise ships requires a multi-faceted approach that includes increasing sanitation and hygiene protocols, providing education to passengers and crew members on how to prevent the spread of the virus, and implementing measures to quickly identify and respond to outbreaks. The CDC recommends that cruise ships take several steps to prevent norovirus outbreaks, including:

  • Increasing sanitation and hygiene protocols, including frequent cleaning and disinfection of surfaces and high-touch areas
  • Providing education to passengers and crew members on how to prevent the spread of norovirus, including proper hand hygiene and food handling practices
  • Implementing measures to quickly identify and respond to outbreaks, including reporting of gastrointestinal illnesses to the ship's medical staff

Conclusion

The Star Princess norovirus outbreak highlights the importance of sanitation and hygiene protocols on cruise ships, and the need for prompt action to contain and prevent the spread of illnesses. The CDC's investigation and response to the outbreak demonstrate the agency's commitment to protecting the health and safety of cruise ship passengers and crew members. By understanding the causes of norovirus outbreaks and taking steps to prevent them, we can reduce the risk of illness and ensure a safe and healthy cruise experience for all.
