2026 BMW M5 Revealed: Hybrid Power & Ultimate Track-Ready Luxury

By {{AUTHOR_NAME}} Senior Editor
Published: May 08, 2026 Updated: May 08, 2026 7 Min Read

The unveiling of the 2026 BMW M5 marks a significant milestone in the model's 40-year history, as it introduces the first-ever plug-in hybrid powertrain to the iconic performance sedan. This bold move combines the traditional thunderous V8 muscle with the instant torque delivery of an electric motor, resulting in a staggering 717 horsepower and 738 lb-ft of torque. To put this into perspective, the new M5 accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in a mere 3.4 seconds for the sedan and 3.5 seconds for the M5 Touring wagon variant, numbers that approach supercar territory.

This paradigm shift in performance is made possible by the combination of a 4.4-litre twin-turbocharged V8 engine and a 14.8 kWh battery pack paired with an electric motor. The power is routed through an eight-speed M Steptronic transmission to all four wheels via M xDrive all-wheel drive, although drivers have the option to switch to pure rear-wheel drive for track use. Additionally, the M5 boasts an EPA-rated 25 miles of pure electric driving range, with the upgraded 11 kW Level 2 charging capability for 2026 reducing recharge times compared to its predecessor.

Key Specs and Features Breakdown

A closer look at the 2026 BMW M5 reveals a host of impressive features and specs. The exterior is characterized by distinctively flared wheel arches, quad exhaust finishers, and the available Iconic Glow kidney grille lighting, giving the M5 a subtly aggressive stance. Inside, the curved digital display integrates a 12.3-inch instrument cluster with a 14.9-inch central touchscreen, running BMW's latest iDrive 8.5 system with M-specific performance displays. Standard equipment includes Merino leather upholstery, Bowers and Wilkins surround sound, four-zone climate control, and M sport seats, ensuring a luxurious and performance-oriented driving experience.

The M5's interior and exterior design elements work in harmony to create a vehicle that is both track-ready and luxuriously appointed. With its advanced technology, including the iDrive 8.5 system, and premium materials, such as Merino leather, the M5 offers an unparalleled driving experience. Whether on the road or on the track, the M5's unique blend of performance, luxury, and technology makes it a compelling choice for driving enthusiasts.

The 2026 BMW M5's specs and features make it an attractive option for those seeking a high-performance vehicle with the latest technology and luxurious amenities. With its powerful hybrid powertrain, advanced all-wheel-drive system, and premium interior, the M5 is well-equipped to handle a variety of driving scenarios, from daily commuting to track days.

How It Compares to the Previous M5

In comparison to its predecessor, the 2026 BMW M5 offers significant improvements in terms of power, efficiency, and technology. The new hybrid powertrain provides a substantial boost in horsepower and torque, making it the most powerful production M5 in the model's history. Additionally, the M5's electric driving range and recharge times have been improved, making it a more practical choice for daily driving. The latest iDrive 8.5 system and premium interior features further enhance the M5's appeal, making it a compelling option for those seeking a high-performance luxury sedan.

The M5's advancements are not limited to its powertrain and technology; its design has also undergone significant changes. The subtly aggressive exterior, complete with flared wheel arches and quad exhaust finishers, gives the M5 a distinctive appearance that sets it apart from its predecessor. The available Iconic Glow kidney grille lighting adds an extra touch of sophistication, making the M5 a head-turner on the road.

Performance — What Tests Show on Road and Track

On the road, the 2026 BMW M5 delivers exceptional performance, with its hybrid powertrain providing instant torque and effortless acceleration. The eight-speed M Steptronic transmission and M xDrive all-wheel-drive system work in harmony to provide precise handling and traction, making the M5 a joy to drive on twisty roads. On the track, the M5's rear-wheel-drive mode allows drivers to fully exploit the vehicle's performance capabilities, with the electric motor providing an added boost of power and torque.

Test drives have shown that the M5's performance is not just limited to straight-line acceleration; it also handles exceptionally well on the track. The M5's advanced suspension and chassis tuning provide a balance of comfort and agility, making it a capable handler on both the road and the track. With its powerful hybrid powertrain and advanced all-wheel-drive system, the M5 is well-suited for a variety of driving scenarios, from daily commuting to track days.
