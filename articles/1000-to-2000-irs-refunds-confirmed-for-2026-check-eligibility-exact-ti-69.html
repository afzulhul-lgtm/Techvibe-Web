What are the Eligibility Criteria for the $1,000 to $2,000 IRS Refund?

To be eligible for the $1,000 to $2,000 IRS refund, taxpayers must meet certain criteria. These include filing a tax return for the 2026 tax year, having a taxable income below $50,000 for single filers or $100,000 for joint filers, and having at least one qualifying child or dependent. Additionally, taxpayers must have paid more in taxes than they owed during the 2026 tax year.

The IRS has also announced that taxpayers who received advance payments of the Child Tax Credit in 2026 may be eligible for an additional refund. This credit, which was introduced as part of the American Rescue Plan, provides eligible families with up to $3,000 per child. Taxpayers who received advance payments of this credit may be eligible for an additional refund of up to $1,500 per child.

How to Check Your Eligibility

To check your eligibility for the $1,000 to $2,000 IRS refund, you can use the IRS's online tool, which can be found on the IRS website. This tool will ask you a series of questions about your income, filing status, and dependents, and will provide you with an estimate of your refund amount. You can also contact the IRS directly to ask about your eligibility and to get more information about the refund process.
