Tiny Startup Humiliates Nvidia and AMD: Runs 700B AI Models on a $0 Cloud Bill Using Decade-Old Chips

By Rachel Wong Senior Editor
Published: May 11, 2026 Updated: May 11, 2026 9 Min Read

The Skymizer HTX301, a PCIe AI accelerator card, has been unveiled, and it's making waves in the tech world. This card can run language models with up to 700 billion parameters on a single device while consuming only 240 watts of power. A skymizer htx301 review is necessary to understand its implications.

So, why does this matter right now? The answer lies in the fact that running large language models in the cloud can be costly and raise privacy concerns. Skymizer's card offers a solution that fits into standard air-cooled servers without any infrastructure changes, making it a **convincing choice** for enterprises.

What Is the Skymizer HTX301 and Why the AI World Is Stunned

The Skymizer HTX301 is built on Skymizer's HyperThought platform, which features next-generation LPU IP designed specifically for large language model workloads. Each PCIe card contains six HTX301 chips working together and offers up to 384 GB of total memory capacity. From what's been reported, the card delivers 30 tokens per second with 0.5 TOPS at 100 GB per second bandwidth.

What that means, practically speaking, is that the HTX301 can support agentic AI for coding, automation, and domain-specific workflows without needing hyperscale GPU clusters. That's where it gets interesting, as this could dramatically lower the barrier to entry for on-premises AI infrastructure. Based on available information, it seems that Skymizer has made a genuine breakthrough.

Here's the thing: the HTX301 achieves this using older 28-nanometer chips and standard LPDDR4 and LPDDR5 memory instead of the expensive HBM or GDDR solutions used by Nvidia and AMD. The design uses efficient compression techniques for both weights and KV cache, outperforming open source llama.cpp by 9 to 17.8 percent. It's hard to say exactly how this will play out in the market, but it's certainly a promising development.

HTX301 Full Specs: 384GB Memory at Just 240 Watts

The HTX301's specs are impressive on paper, with up to 384 GB of total memory capacity and a power consumption of less than 240 watts. To put that in perspective, Nvidia's RTX PRO 6000 Blackwell consumes roughly 600 watts, more than double what Skymizer's card requires for comparable inference tasks. And, the HTX301 supports up to 700 billion parameter LLMs on a single card, making it a formidable option for enterprises.

Worth noting is that the HTX301 uses a combination of LPDDR4 and LPDDR5 memory, which is a more cost-effective solution than the HBM or GDDR memory used by Nvidia and AMD. The card's efficient design and use of older chip architecture make it a strong alternative for businesses looking to reduce their power consumption and costs. From a quarterly earnings perspective, this could be a notable advantage for companies looking to invest in AI infrastructure.

On the flip side, it's not entirely clear yet how the HTX301 will perform in real-world testing. That's where the upcoming Computex event comes in, where Skymizer will preview the HTX301, allowing for a closer look at its performance.
